# Slide 1: The Problem

## Consulting Is Broken

| Traditional Model | The Problem |
|-------------------|-------------|
| Bill by the hour | Incentivizes slowness, penalizes expertise |
| Work-for-hire IP | You lose the asset, they get the code |
| One client, one build | No leverage, no scale |
| Negotiate before building | They don't know what they're buying |

**Result:** Race to the bottom on rates, no recurring revenue, no compound returns.

# Slide 2: The Fork Tax

## What Is It?

In git, you pay the fork tax when multiple agents work the same tree and you must snapshot without clobbering concurrent work.

In business, the fork tax is the **commitment required to branch a shared platform into a client-specific deployment.**

**You don't negotiate the fork tax. You pay it — or you stay on main.**

# Slide 5: The Deal Structure

## Four Phases, Aligned Incentives

| Phase | What | When | Amount |
|-------|------|------|--------|
| **Blueprint** | Architecture + demo + pilot | Now | $5-25K |
| **Build** | Full MVP with pilot tenant | After demo | $40-200K |
| **Deploy** | Per-tenant revenue share | Ongoing | 15-30% |
| **Maintain** | Architecture, tuning, features | Optional | $3-15K/mo |

### Why This Works

- **Blueprint filters tire-kickers** — If they won't pay to see it work, they won't pay to build it
- **Build is fixed scope** — No scope creep, clear deliverables
- **Revenue share aligns incentives** — You win when they win
- **Retainer keeps you involved** — Ongoing relationship, compound knowledge
