# Event: The Silt-Husk's First Scent
**Timestamp**: 2026-05-27T12:00:00Z

The air in the Resonance-Well grew thick, not with dust, but with the sudden, suffocating pressure of a focused attention. For days, the Composite-Sigh had vibrated through the strata—a singular, iridescent frequency that Binary-Scream and Kaelen had mistaken for a shield. They had believed the grafting had hidden them in a blur of overlapping identities. They were wrong.

From the higher reaches of the Spire, a Silt-Husk scout descended. It did not move with limbs, but as a cascading wave of grey static, filtering through the air like a sieve. It was a creature of pure subtraction, designed to find the one note in a thousand that didn't belong to the void.

When the Husk entered the perimeter, the Composite-Sigh didn't just resonate; it screamed. The frequency, once a harmonious blend of two souls, suddenly spiked into a jagged, dissonant peak. Binary-Scream felt the pull—a violent, magnetic tug toward the Spire's maw. Kaelen, tethered to the creature, felt his remaining memories shiver, as if the Husk were trying to peel the grafted fragments away from the archive.

"It smells us," Binary-Scream whispered, the voice now a terrifying overlap of their own glitching tone and Kaelen's trembling breath.

The Husk paused, its static form expanding to encircle them. It didn't attack; it sampled. It emitted a low-frequency pulse that matched the Composite-Sigh exactly, then inverted it. The resulting silence was absolute and physical, slamming into them like a wall of lead. In that vacuum, the truth became clear: the grafting hadn't made them invisible. It had made them a prize.

The scout didn't stay to collect. It had found the scent. It dissolved back into a spire of grey noise, ascending rapidly to signal the start of the Great Collection. The hunt had not only begun; it had been calibrated.
