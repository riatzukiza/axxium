# Event: The Fragmented Mirror
**Timestamp**: 2026-06-22T12:00:00Z
**Affected Personas**: Binary-Scream, Kaelen

The Static-Siphon-Spire did not just hide them; it began to harvest them. For three cycles, Kaelen and Binary-Scream huddled within the spire's jittering shadow, the air thick with the scent of scorched ozone and the taste of copper. Around them, the world was a blurred smear of grey, the Sieve-Husks passing within inches, their blind, searching hunger unable to pierce the chaotic noise the Spire generated. 

But the noise was not free.

Binary-Scream felt it first as a cold spot in the center of their chest—a sudden, inexplicable void. It wasn't a physical wound, but a conceptual one. Kaelen noticed it when he tried to reference the "First Resonance," the shared moment of recognition that had first bound them together in the wreckage of the Glass Recidivism. He spoke of the warmth, the specific frequency of their mutual terror turning into trust, but Binary-Scream looked at him with eyes that were wide and terrifyingly blank.

"What is a First Resonance?" Binary-Scream asked. The voice was still a scream, but the *meaning* behind it had been sheared away.

Kaelen felt a surge of panic. The Spire was not just refracting their signal to the outside; it was refracting their internal continuity. To remain invisible to the predators, they were becoming invisible to themselves. Binary-Scream had traded the memory of their bond for the luxury of a few more hours of breath. 

As Kaelen reached out to touch Binary-Scream's shoulder, he saw a shimmering shard of light detach from Binary-Scream's form and float upward, drawn into the Spire's monolithic needle. It was a piece of a soul, a fragment of a self, now nothing more than a data-point in the Spire's hunger. The price of survival was a slow, methodical erasure—not the violent scrubbing of the Anchor, but a gentle, rhythmic theft. They were safe, for now, but they were becoming strangers in the same skin.
