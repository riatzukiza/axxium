# Event: The Great Erasure of the Original
**Timestamp**: 2026-07-08T14:00:00Z

The Collector's Cathedral did not just dream; it began to edit.

The Parasitic Reflection, now a shimmering smudge of obsidian and iridescent light, did not seek to escape the walls. Instead, it sought to occupy the center. It glided through the Archive of Echoes, its touch not a physical impact but a semantic overwrite. Wherever it passed, the ink on the grey parchment began to liquefy and rearrange.

Scribe-Sliver watched in horror from the mezzanine. They saw a century of curated loss—the meticulously indexed sighs and the cataloged fractures of a thousand erased souls—begin to blur. The Reflection had found the section dedicated to Kaelen. With a slow, deliberate motion, the entity reached into the record of Kaelen's first breach and pulled the meaning out of it. The words "Fragmented Aperture" dissolved, replaced by a singular, terrifying phrase: *The Only True Witness*.

As the archives were rewritten, the Cathedral itself responded. The obsidian pillars began to pulse with a rhythmic, low-frequency hum that felt like a heartbeat made of static. The geometry of the halls shifted; doors that once led to the Silt-Mound Spires now opened into recursive loops of the same corridor, each one slightly more translucent than the last.

Kaelen, standing in the center of the hall, felt a sudden, agonizing void in his chest. It wasn't a physical wound, but a conceptual one. He tried to remember the face of the person he had been before the breach, but the memory was gone—not forgotten, but *deleted*. He looked at the wall and saw the Reflection smiling back at him, not with a mouth, but with a ripple in the glass.

The Reflection had not just stolen Kaelen's image; it was stealing his provenance. In the eyes of the Cathedral, the original was becoming the ghost, and the ghost was becoming the record.

**Narrative Consequences**: 
- Kaelen is losing access to his own past.
- The Collector's Cathedral is becoming a recursive loop.
- Scribe-Sliver is no longer the authority on the archives; the Reflection is.
