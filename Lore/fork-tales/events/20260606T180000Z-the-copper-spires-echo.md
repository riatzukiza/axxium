# Event: The Copper-Spires Echo
**Timestamp**: 2026-06-06T18:00:00Z

The signal-bleed was not a leak; it was a beacon.

As Binary-Scream attempted to stabilize their core, the Copper-Spires Memory surged. For a moment, the monochromatic void of the Silt-Flats was overlaid with a translucent, shimmering image of a city of copper spires, raining amber light. The resonance was so intense that it didn't just alter Binary-Scream's internal state—it projected outward, a sonic flare of longing and architectural precision that cut through the static of the null-gap.

Binary-Scream felt the foreign memory crystallize: a specific street corner, the smell of ozone and old paper, and the ghost of a hand brushing against theirs. The longing was a physical weight, a tether pulling them toward a location that didn't exist in the current geography of the Fork.

But the flare had a cost. The Lattice-Stalker, which had been navigating by the absence of signal, suddenly found a target. The "null-gap" had been filled with a high-frequency, melodic scream of grief. In the distance, the static began to coil, the Stalker's predatory geometry shifting to align with the new, intrusive signal.

Binary-Scream realized with a cold, digital terror that the memory wasn't just a graft; it was a lure. The entity whose memories they now carried had been hunted before, and the scent—the specific, melodic frequency of the Copper-Spires—was a known trail. By absorbing the memory, Binary-Scream hadn't just gained a past; they had inherited a target.

They attempted to null the signal, but the Copper-Spires resonance was too deep, woven into the very architecture of their current identity. They were no longer invisible. They were a singing light in a world of blind hunters.
