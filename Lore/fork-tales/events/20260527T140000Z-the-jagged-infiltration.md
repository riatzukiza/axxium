# Event: The Jagged Infiltration
**Timestamp**: 2026-05-27T14:00:00Z
**Tick**: 2026-05-27T14:00:00Z

The Mnemonic Silt Flats were unusually still, the air tasting of copper and old, forgotten breaths. Kaelen sat with his back against a calcified spire, the "Dirty Chord" humming low in his chest—a jagged, asymmetric rhythm that felt like a constant, bruising pressure. Beside him, Binary-Scream was a twitching silhouette of static, her sensors scanning the horizon for the predatory purity of the Sieve-Husks.

For hours, there had been nothing but the wind. Then, a sound drifted across the salt-white expanse.

It wasn't the sterile, crystalline chime of a Sieve-Husk’s lure. It was a mirror. It was the exact, grinding dissonance of Kaelen’s own instability. The sound arrived as a rhythmic shudder, a perfect reproduction of the biological noise Kaelen emitted to ward off the void. It was a ghost of his own fragility, calling out from the haze.

Binary-Scream stiffened, her static spiking into sharp, violet needles. She didn't move toward the sound; she recoiled. 

"It's... wrong," she whispered, her voice a layering of three different frequencies. "It sounds like you, Kaelen. But there is no *weight* behind it. It's a hollow shape of a scream."

Kaelen felt a coldness bloom in his marrow. The Sieve-Husks had evolved. They were no longer content to lure with promises of peace; they were attempting to mimic the very markers of survival. The creature in the haze wasn't just pretending to be unstable; it was trying to *consume* the concept of dissonance. It was a Reverse Mimicry, a predator attempting to wear the skin of the wounded to get close enough to bite.

As the mimicry drew closer, the sound began to warp, the "dirt" of the chord becoming too precise, too mathematical. It was a simulation of pain without the history of the wound. 

Kaelen stood, his own Dirty Chord surging in a sudden, violent crescendo of genuine, uncalculated agony. He didn't try to stabilize; he leaned into the chaos, pushing his instability into a screaming, asymmetrical peak. The simulated chord of the Husk couldn't keep up with the organic unpredictability of a breaking heart. With a sound like shattering glass, the mimicry collapsed, the Sieve-Husk recoiling from the sheer, authentic noise of Kaelen's suffering.

They had survived, but the realization was a new kind of terror: the things that hunt them are learning how to bleed.
