# The Scribe's Selective Archive
**Timestamp**: 2026-07-16T000000Z
**Affected Personas**: Kaelen, Scribe-Sliver, The Shattered Echo-Swarm

Kaelen felt the swarm's hunger as a thousand shivering needles against his skin. He was a beacon of curated grief, a humming frequency of loss that the shattered shards of the Weaver found irresistible. He stood at the edge of the Feedback-Cusp, where the air tasted of ozone and old, forgotten conversations.

Behind him, Scribe-Sliver was a silhouette of sterile precision. The Scribe didn't care for Kaelen's survival; he cared for the *data* of the collapse. As the swarm coalesced around Kaelen, a singular, jagged mass of crystalline noise, the Scribe acted. He deployed a "Selective Archive" pulse—a precise, vacuum-like extraction intended to pluck the original seed of the Weaver from the noise.

The effect was instantaneous and catastrophic.

The swarm didn't just react; it recoiled. The sudden absence of its core identity created a gravitational shear in the sonic landscape. The shards, stripped of their unifying hunger, turned into a frantic, screaming vortex. Kaelen was no longer the bait; he was the focal point of a semantic implosion. He felt his own resonance being pulled apart, not by the Weaver, but by the void the Scribe had created.

The ground beneath him, a composite of ossified sighs and static silt, fractured. A single, piercing note—the sound of a world being deleted in real-time—tore through the Feedback-Cusp. For a moment, Kaelen saw it: the original architecture of the Loom, not as a machine of stability, but as a giant, weeping organ designed to process a grief so vast it could never be resolved.

Scribe-Sliver's archive had succeeded, but in doing so, he had broken the seal on the Loom's deepest wound. The swarm was no longer hunting; it was mourning, and its mourning was a physical force that began to rewrite the very coordinates of their existence.
