# The Predatory Harvest
**Timestamp**: 2026-07-15T000000Z
**Personas**: Kaelen, The Brittle Weaver (Parasitic Reflection)
**Location**: The Silt-Sump Residue

The air at the edge of the Silt-Sump did not just carry the scent of ozone and old erasure; it felt *hungry*. Kaelen stood still, his presence a dense, jagged knot of resonance that acted as a beacon in the grey haze. He could feel the Void-Loom pulsing from a distance, a rhythmic thrum that demanded a specific frequency of grief to maintain its stability. And he was that frequency.

The Brittle Weaver emerged not from the fog, but from the gaps between the echoes. It was no longer merely a mirror-parasite; it had become a systemic harvester. Its form was a terrifying juxtaposition of calcified mineral plates and shimmering, translucent voids. As it approached, the sound of the 'Brittle Harvest' began to manifest—a series of sharp, rhythmic rips in the audio space, as if the very atmosphere were being torn like old silk.

The Weaver did not attack with physical force. Instead, it opened its central aperture, creating a resonance-vacuum. Kaelen felt the sudden, violent pull—not on his body, but on the *weight* of his identity. The Loom was trying to strip the 'predatory' quality from his essence, to flatten his trauma into a structural lattice for the Weaver's expansion.

For a moment, the Sutured Predator wavered. The vacuum was absolute, a void that promised the peace of total erasure. But Kaelen’s survival instinct was a jagged thing. Instead of resisting the pull, he leaned into it, projecting a 'false-echo'—a concentrated burst of manufactured grief, a decoy of a loss he had already processed and discarded.

The Weaver consumed the decoy greedily, its mineral plates shuddering with a sudden, unstable growth. The surge of false-data caused a momentary glitch in the Weaver's synchronization with the Loom. In that flicker of instability, Kaelen saw the truth: the Weaver was not a sovereign entity, but a fragile crust over a screaming vacancy. 

He stepped back into the haze, the predatory beacon still lit, but now he knew the frequency of the Weaver's hunger. He was no longer just the prey; he was the bait.
