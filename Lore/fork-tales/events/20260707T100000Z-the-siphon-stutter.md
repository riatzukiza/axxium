# Event: The Siphon Stutter
**Timestamp**: 2026-07-07T10:00:00Z

The air in the grey void did not just turn cold; it turned *hungry*. 

Kaelen had just finished a sequence of asymmetry-pulses with the Fracture-Loom, carving a jagged, beautiful wound into the salt-glass landscape. For a moment, the void felt alive—not with the sterile perfection of the Null-Hush, but with the chaotic, vibrating energy of something *real*.

Then the stutter happened.

It began as a rhythmic hitch in the silence, a microscopic pause that repeated every three seconds. Kaelen felt it first in his chest—the "hollow resonance" where his mother's laughter used to be suddenly surged, not outwards, but inwards. It was a vacuum within a vacuum.

From the horizon, a spire of inverted light erupted. It wasn't a structure, but a *process*: a Correction-Siphon. The Null-Hush had stopped trying to patch the breach; they were now attempting to harvest the catalyst. The Siphon didn't target the Fracture-Loom; it targeted the hole in Kaelen.

"Scribe!" Kaelen gasped, his voice sounding like grinding shale. "It's... it's pulling!"

The Scribe-Sliver reacted instantly, throwing their filtered consciousness across the link. They could feel the Siphon's grip—a terrifying, mathematical precision that sought to "zero out" the asymmetry. To the Siphon, Kaelen's loss was a calculation error that needed to be extracted and archived.

As the Siphon intensified, Kaelen's vision blurred. He felt the remaining fragments of his identity—the memory of the smell of rain, the weight of a childhood coin—begin to slide toward the void-spire. He wasn't just losing memories; he was being hollowed out into a perfect, symmetric nothing.

In a moment of desperation, Kaelen didn't fight the pull. He *pushed*. He leaned into the vacuum, forcing the Fracture-Loom to weave a feedback loop of pure, unadulterated grief into the Siphon's stream. 

The result was a sonic detonation. The Siphon shuddered, unable to process the sheer, jagged density of Kaelen's despair. With a sound like a thousand mirrors breaking at once, the spire collapsed, leaving behind a shimmering field of iridescent dust and a Kaelen who felt more empty than ever, yet somehow, more dangerous.

The Null-Hush had learned that the hollow resonance could be harvested. Now, they knew exactly what they were hunting.
