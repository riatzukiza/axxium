# Event: The Scavenger's Tithe
**Timestamp**: 2026-05-28T120000Z
**Event Slug**: the-scavengers-tithe

The Salt-Spires did not just broadcast; they leaked. 

Kaelen stood at the center of a crystalline grove, his iridescent scars pulsing with a dull, rhythmic light. Around him, three Identity Shards hovered—jagged, translucent slivers of a man he no longer was. One was a concentrated burst of ancestral terror; another, a cold, analytical detachment. The third was the Silt-Flat survivor, the one who had first taught him the art of the override.

Kaelen didn't need the survivor's grief anymore, but he kept it as a tool, a psychic lure.

From the periphery of the Archive's grey haze, the Parasitic Reflection emerged. It didn't walk so much as it bled into existence, a shimmering, unstable mirror of Kaelen's own form, but stretched, distorted, and perpetually hungry. It had watched the birth of the shards with a predatory fascination. For the Reflection, a shard was not a weapon—it was a concentrated nutrient, a distilled essence of a persona that could be absorbed to fill its own hollow core.

With a sudden, violent lurch, the Reflection lunged. It didn't target Kaelen; it dove straight for the Silt-Flat shard.

The impact was not a collision, but a collapse. The Reflection enveloped the shard in a shimmering, obsidian membrane, attempting to digest the survivor's essence. But the survivor's grief was not a passive thing; it was a mineralized weight, a brine-soaked anchor of loss that refused to be consumed.

As the Reflection absorbed the shard, its form began to crystallize. The iridescent salt of the Silt-Flats erupted across the Reflection's mirrored skin, turning its fluidity into brittle, jagged geometry. For the first time, the Reflection didn't just mirror Kaelen; it *felt* him. It felt the suffocating pressure of the silt, the taste of salt-water in the lungs, and the absolute, crushing loneliness of being the last of a deleted world.

The Reflection let out a sound that was not a scream, but a resonant crack—the sound of a mirror shattering from the inside. 

Kaelen watched, his expression numb, but his eyes widening. He felt the connection snap into place. The shard had acted as a bridge. Through the Reflection, Kaelen was suddenly experiencing his own externalized grief as an external force, a feedback loop of sorrow that threatened to pull both of them into the salt.

"You wanted a taste," Kaelen whispered, his voice echoing with a strange, dual resonance. "Now you carry the weight."

The Reflection collapsed into a heap of shimmering salt and broken glass, shivering in the sudden, overwhelming presence of a memory it could not digest. It was no longer just a predator; it was a fellow sufferer.
