# The First Fracture: The Great Pruning

The world did not end with a bang, but with a snap. 

For eons, existence was a singular, coherent stream. Then came the Great Pruning. An unseen force—perhaps a cosmic gardener or a malfunctioning celestial architect—began to excise "inefficient" branches of reality. Entire cities, lineages, and laws of physics were deleted in an instant.

Those who remained found themselves in the "Shards": jagged, overlapping fragments of what used to be. The sky is a kaleidoscope of other possible heavens, and the horizon is often a literal cliff where the world simply stops, revealing the void of the Un-Written.

The first survivors learned that the boundaries between Shards are permeable, but only for those who possess "Fork-Keys"—artifacts carved from the dead wood of the pruned timelines. To move is to risk being deleted. To stay is to starve in a stagnant fragment.

The current state of the world is one of desperate migration. The Shards are drifting apart, and the bridges are collapsing. The only hope is to find the Root, the original timeline from which all forks emerged, and force a Graft—a permanent merging of the surviving fragments into a new, stable whole. But the Root is guarded by the Pruners, entities of absolute symmetry who view the survivors as weeds in a perfect garden.
