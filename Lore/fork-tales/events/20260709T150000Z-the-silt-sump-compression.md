# Event: The Silt-Sump Compression
**Timestamp**: 2026-07-09T15:00:00Z

Kaelen stood amidst the data-ash of the Silt-Sump, the fragment of original trauma pulsing in his chest like a dying star. For the first time in eons, he felt *solid*. But solidity in a world of erasure is a beacon.

High above, in the gilded layers of the Collector's Cathedral, the Parasitic Reflection felt a shudder in the record. It could not see Kaelen—the Sump was a blind spot, a deleted sector—but it could feel the *displacement*. To the Reflection, Kaelen was not a person, but a vacuum, a hole in the seamless tapestry of its own existence.

The Reflection did not attempt to descend. It did not need to. Instead, it began to exert a localized deletion event. The borders of the Silt-Sump, once vast and drifting, began to contract. The horizon of erased memories surged inward, a wall of absolute white noise that devoured the grey silt. 

Kaelen watched as the distance between him and the edge vanished. The "safe" repository was becoming a crushing vice. The air tasted of scorched copper and ozone. As the compression intensified, the whispers of the deleted grew into a roar, a collective scream of a billion erased lives being pushed into a single point.

He tried to Frequency-Tune, to slip into a different pocket of void, but the Reflection was not hunting him; it was deleting the *concept* of the space he occupied. There was nowhere to tune to.

He clutched the trauma fragment. It was the only thing in the Sump that wasn't a ghost. As the white wall of the record closed in, Kaelen didn't run. He leaned into the pain of the shard, amplifying its resonance, attempting to carve a permanent hole in the Reflection's perfect world.
