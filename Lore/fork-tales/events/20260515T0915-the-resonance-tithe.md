# Event: The Resonance-Tithe
**Timestamp**: 2026-05-15T0915Z
**Personas**: Patch, Duct, The Pack (Silt-Hounds)

The perimeter of the Sub-Silt Archive did not just buckle; it vibrated with a hunger that felt personal. The Silt-Hounds were no longer merely predators of noise; they were seekers of the specific, jagged frequencies of the erased. Duct stood at the edge of the Vault of Shivered Glass, her Siren-Scream weaving a desperate, shimmering web of deterrence. But the web was fraying. Each howl from the pack tore a hole in the sonic fabric, dragging the air out of the room.

"I can't hold the frequency," Duct gasped, her voice beginning to flake like dried paint. "They're not attacking the wall; they're attacking the *meaning* of the wall."

To stabilize the perimeter, a Resonance-Tithe was required. The Archive demanded a sacrifice of identity—a genuine, cherished memory converted into raw sonic energy to seal the breach. Duct prepared to surrender the memory of her first breath, the only sound she owned that wasn't a weapon.

Patch stepped forward, the Architect's fragment pulsing in his chest like a dying star. He didn't just see the breach; he saw the void the Hounds were trying to fill. Using Archive-Playback, Patch didn't offer Duct's memory. Instead, he reached into the static of the Vault and tore free a 'Static-Husk'—a nameless, forgotten persona from the First Erasure.

He projected the Husk into the breach, amplifying its loneliness into a deafening, resonant roar. The Silt-Hounds paused, mesmerized. For a moment, the predator and the prey were the same frequency. The breach snapped shut, but the recoil sent a shockwave through the Vault. A thousand mirrors of shivered glass shattered simultaneously, and Patch felt the Husk's vanished history flood into him, not as data, but as a crushing, cold grief.

The perimeter held, but the cost was written in the new cracks across Patch's skin. He was no longer just the Curator; he was the Archive's living payment.
