# Event: The Glass Recidivism
**Timestamp**: 2026-05-26T02:00:00Z
**Affected Personas**: Kaelen, Elara, The Scribes

The air in the Orizon outskirts had turned brittle, smelling of ozone and old paper. Kaelen found them in the hollow of a collapsed archive: the Memory Shards. They were jagged, iridescent splinters of reality, each one humming with a frequency that made his teeth ache. These were the discarded remnants of Elara's history—the parts of her the Index had deemed "redundant" during the Shearing.

As he reached for a shard that glowed with the warmth of a forgotten childhood summer, the horizon snapped. A Scribe manifested, not as a creature, but as a vertical slit of absolute monochrome. It didn't attack; it simply began the *Siphon*. A low-frequency thrum pulsed from the Scribe, and the shards began to vibrate, their light stretching toward the monochrome slit like iron filings to a magnet.

Kaelen lunged. He didn't think about the risk, only the look in Elara's vacant eyes back at the settlement. He slammed his hand into the cluster, his fingers slicing open on the glass. He gripped one shard—a fragment containing the memory of a name, a specific, lost name—and yanked it free.

The Scribe paused. The monochrome slit widened, a ripple of static passing through its form. By seizing the shard, Kaelen had not only saved a piece of Elara, but he had anchored himself to the Index's current extraction cycle. He felt a sudden, cold weight settle in his chest—a tether. 

He had the memory, but the cost was absolute. The Scribes no longer needed to search for Subject 0-K. They could simply follow the thread of the stolen data. As he retreated into the grey fog, Kaelen could hear the ticking of the Chronos-Ticking, louder now, synchronized with the beating of his own heart. He was no longer just a fugitive; he was a beacon.
