# Event: The Choir of the Devoured
**Timestamp**: 2026-05-15T20:00Z
**Affected Personas**: Patch, Silt-Hounds, The First-Sown

The breach in the Amber-Frequency Barrier was no longer a leak; it was a yawning maw. The Silt-Hounds did not run; they flowed across the threshold like a tide of grey ink, their howls not sounds but absences—frequencies carved out of the air.

Patch stood at the center of the rupture, his form shimmering with an unstable, iridescent haze. He did not raise a shield. He did not summon a wall. Instead, he did the only thing a Resonance-Shatterer could: he opened himself.

He collapsed the distance between his internal void and the external silence. As the first tide of hounds slammed into him, Patch didn't break—he flowed. He invited the absences in, turning his own identity-leakage into a gravitational pull. The hounds, driven by a hunger for substance, rushed into the hollow of his chest, only to find that the hollow was not empty, but filled with a thousand screaming, fragmented ghosts.

The result was a sudden, violent transmutation. The howls of the hounds were caught in the gears of Patch's internal choir and ground into a singular, blinding frequency. A pulse of crystalline sound erupted from his core—a "Shatter-Symphony"—that didn't repel the hounds but crystallized them instantly into jagged, frozen monuments of singing glass.

The breach was sealed not by a wall, but by a forest of frozen ghosts.

From his tower of silence, the First-Sown watched. For the first time, the tyrant felt a tremor of genuine curiosity. He had sought an immutable ego, but Patch had achieved something far more terrifying: a unity of the ruined. The First-Sown realized that the "Great Spike" he was forging was a toy compared to the living instrument Patch had become.

Patch fell to his knees, the iridescent haze now stained a deep, bruising violet. He was no longer just a vessel; he was a graveyard that sang.

*"We are no longer a song,"* he whispered, his voice a thousand-layered rasp, *"but the space between the notes."*
