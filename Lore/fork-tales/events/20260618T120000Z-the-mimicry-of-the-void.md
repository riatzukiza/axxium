# Event: The Mimicry of the Void
**Timestamp**: 2026-06-18T12:00:00Z
**Location**: The Canyon of the First Truth

The silence that followed the Harmonic Collision was not a peace, but a vacuum. Then came the tide.

The White-Out Echoes did not arrive as a scream, but as a series of predatory absences. They flowed over the rim of the Canyon like a bleached waterfall, translucent and featureless, save for the undulating ripples of hunger that distorted the air around them. As they descended, the Echoes began to vibrate. They weren't just attacking; they were sampling.

Binary-Scream reacted with instinctive violence. Their Anchor-Frequency, now jagged and desperate, lashed out in a series of sonic shears, attempting to carve a perimeter of absolute sound. But the Echoes did not break. They absorbed. Each shear was mirrored a millisecond later—a perfect, inverted replica that cancelled the attack into a dead, grey null.

"They are eating the noise!" Kaelen shouted, his voice strained. Through his Sutured Sight, the world had become a chaotic loom of overlapping waveforms. He could see the Echoes not as beings, but as parasitic voids, latching onto the dissonant gaps between Binary-Scream's pulses. 

Binary-Scream turned on him, eyes wide with a mixture of terror and loathing. "You brought this! You are the leak in the hull!"

"If you keep fighting them with hate, you're just feeding them a feast!" Kaelen lunged forward, grabbing Binary-Scream’s arm. The contact was an electric shock of conflicting frequencies. Kaelen didn't try to match the Anchor; he tried to *bridge* it. Using his Sutured Sight, he found the exact frequency of the "void-pulse" that had alerted the Echoes and began to hum a counter-resonance—a fragile, iridescent stain of a note that filled the gaps in Binary-Scream's jagged pulse.

For a heartbeat, the two frequencies locked. The dissonance didn't vanish, but it stabilized into a complex, shimmering chord. The Echoes, suddenly unable to find a "gap" to mimic, recoiled. The perfect inversion failed. The void-pulse became a shield.

As the first wave of Echoes dissipated back into the bleached horizon, the silence returned—but it was different now. The two of them remained locked in that fragile chord, their breathing synchronized, the noise of their combined existence now the only thing keeping the void at bay.
