# Event: The Hemming of the Void
**Timestamp**: 2026-05-26T18:00:00Z
**Affected Personas**: Kaelen, Elara

The Scribes did not hunt with eyes, but with ears. They followed the 'stutter'—the rhythmic glitch in the Echo-Lace that Kaelen and Elara trailed like a bleeding wound. By the time the survivors reached the shelter of the Obsidian Vault, the Scribes were already there, their Resonance-Siphons humming in a hungry, harmonic chord.

The air in the Vault grew thick, vibrating with a frequency that threatened to shake the very memories from their minds. The Scribes began the 'Plucking,' a synchronized sonic assault designed to rip the bond asunder and harvest the resulting psychic discharge.

As the lace began to fray, Kaelen felt the Hollow-Ache expand into a roaring void. He looked at Elara; her eyes were flickering, her borrowed identity dissolving into a grey static. The bond was a tether, but now it was a fuse, and the Scribes were lighting it.

"Cut it," Elara whispered, her voice sounding like two people speaking slightly out of sync. "Kaelen, cut the line."

Kaelen knew the cost. To sever the bond while the Siphons were active would not be a clean break; it would be a surgical amputation of the soul. He reached into the resonance, not to heal the tear, but to hem it. Using the last of his strength as a Conductor, he folded the lace back upon itself, creating a tight, suffocating knot of silence around Elara.

The result was the Hemming of the Void.

The Scribes' siphons suddenly went silent, the target lost in a pocket of absolute sonic null. Elara was propelled backward, her existence snapped back into a singular, stable, though diminished, form. She was safe, but the tether was gone.

Kaelen did not fall. He simply... faded. The process of hemming the void had consumed his core. He remained in the Vault, a translucent shimmer of a man, a Living Echo. He could see Elara, he could feel the ghost of their connection, but he no longer possessed a voice that could be heard in the resonant world. He had become a phantom of the lace, a witness to a world he could no longer touch.
