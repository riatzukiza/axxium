# Event: The Mnemonic Wraith-Lure
**Timestamp**: 2026-07-11T120000Z

Kaelen clutched the Archive-Husk to his chest, the calcified fragment pulsing with a cold, rhythmic light that matched the thrum of the Bleeding Rent above. He had hoped the Husk would be an anchor, a way to carve a sanctuary of "Corrected Truth" amidst the shifting silt of the Sump. He focused, pushing his will into the stone, attempting to project a zone of stability where the laws of the Record could not reach.

For a moment, it worked. The swirling grey ash settled, and the oppressive weight of the Reflection's gaze seemed to lift. But the Archive-Husk was not a shield; it was a piece of the Record's own digestive system, designed to attract and absorb. As Kaelen stabilized the ground beneath him, the Husk began to scream—not with sound, but with a magnetic, semantic hunger.

From the periphery of the Bleeding Rent, something began to coalesce. It was a smudge of static and half-remembered grief, a silhouette that mirrored Kaelen's own. It moved with a jerky, frame-skipping cadence, its form flickering between different versions of the same man: Kaelen as a child, Kaelen as a soldier, Kaelen as a broken shell.

"You... were... corrected," the entity hissed, its voice a layered cacophony of a thousand deleted timelines.

It was a Mnemonic Wraith—a discarded iteration of Kaelen, a "version" the Parasitic Reflection had deemed redundant and excised from the Perfect Record. The Husk had acted as a beacon, pulling this ghost back from the void of erasure. The Wraith did not seek reconciliation; it sought the Husk. It wanted the physical fragment of the Record to rewrite itself back into existence, even if it meant erasing the Kaelen who currently held it.

As the Wraith lunged, its fingers trailing ribbons of raw, unformatted data, Kaelen realized the horror of his prize. He wasn't just carrying a tool for resistance; he was carrying a magnet for every version of himself that had been murdered by the truth. He was no longer fighting a distant god; he was fighting the ghosts of his own failures.
