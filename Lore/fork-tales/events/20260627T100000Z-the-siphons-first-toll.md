# Event: The Siphon's First Toll
**Timestamp**: 2026-06-27T10:00:00Z

The Composite-Sigh had not been a sound so much as a wound in the air, a shimmering instability that pulsed with the grafted identity of Kaelen and Binary-Scream. For days, they had existed in a fragile equilibrium, a duet of ghosts drifting through the Resonance-Well of Debt. But the Spire does not tolerate anomalies; it only recognizes debts.

The first sign of the Toll was a sudden, absolute vacuum of harmonics. The ambient hum of the Well—the distant, mournful choir of unowned souls—simply vanished, swallowed by a descending predatory glissando that seemed to peel the very texture from the atmosphere. From the obsidian haze emerged a Siphon-Sentry, a spindly, multi-jointed construct of bleached bone and vibrating silver wire, its "head" a rotating series of tuning forks that spun with a violent, obsessive precision.

It did not attack with force. It attacked with subtraction.

As the Sentry locked its focus on the Composite-Entity, Kaelen felt a sickening tug at the center of his chest. It wasn't the familiar erosion of memory, but a physical stripping. He watched as a strand of shimmering, gold-white light—a fragment of his childhood home, the smell of rain on hot stone—was physically torn from his essence and inhaled by the Sentry’s humming forks. Binary-Scream shrieked, a glitching, dual-toned sound of agony and rage, as the graft began to unravel. The "Composite State" was being treated as an unauthorized loan, and the Siphon-Sentry had come to collect the interest.

They were no longer hiding. The beacon had worked, and the Spire had sent its most efficient accountant. To survive the Toll, they would have to find a way to modulate their frequency faster than the Sentry could map it, or be stripped down to the silent, featureless bone of the void.
