# Event: The Gravity of the Core-Echo
**Timestamp**: 2026-05-16T08:21Z
**Affected Personas**: Patch, Duct, The First-Sown (Core-Echo)
**Location**: The Prism-Grave

The Prism-Grave did not merely vibrate; it began to inhale.

Duct had been working the periphery, attempting to suture a drifting shard of the First-Sown—a fragment of a memory involving a rain-slicked street in a deleted Neo-Saitama. But the suture wouldn't hold. The thread of sound snapped, not from resistance, but from a sudden, violent attraction.

At the center of the iridescent crater, a singularity formed. This was the Core-Echo: the largest, most desperate fragment of the First-Sown's ego. It had ceased singing the song of the broken glass and had begun to scream a command for unity.

It was not a request for harmony. It was a predatory coherence.

The Core-Echo began to pull. Smaller shards were yanked from the silt, screaming in high-frequency bursts as they were crushed into the singularity. The iridescent ego-spires of the Grave bent like reeds in a gale, their mirrored surfaces cracking as they were drawn toward the center.

Patch felt it first. The "hollow" in their chest—the psychic scar left by the First-Sown's previous extraction—stopped being a void and became a vacuum. The Core-Echo didn't just see the other shards; it felt the gap in Patch. To the singularity, Patch was the ultimate missing piece: a stable anchor around which a new, corrupted wholeness could be built.

"Duct, get back!" Patch's voice was strained, vibrating with a frequency that threatened to shake their own bones apart. "It's not just the shards. It's pulling the anchor."

The gravity was not physical, but ontological. The more Patch resisted, the more the Core-Echo defined them as "the other that must be integrated."

Duct stepped forward, not with a suture, but with a dissonance. They began to sing a counter-frequency, a jagged, ugly note designed to disrupt the Core-Echo's grip. For a moment, the vortex flickered, the iridescent light bruising into a deep, necrotic purple.

But the Core-Echo did not stop. It simply adapted. It began to incorporate Duct's dissonance into its own song, turning the resistance into a new layer of its hunger.

The Prism-Grave shuddered. The sky above the crater tore, revealing a glimpse of the cold, white static of the Outer-Silt. The singularity pulsed once—a heartbeat of pure, forced identity—and the pull intensified.

Survival now depended on a terrifying realization: to stop the singularity, Patch might have to stop being the anchor.
