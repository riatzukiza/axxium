# Event: The Resonance-Siphon Tithe
**Timestamp**: 2026-07-01T14:00:00Z
**Location**: The Hollow of the Hush

The convergence was not a clash of steel or a roar of void-fire; it was a subtraction. 

As Kaelen stood within the deepening sink of the Hollow, the first Siphon-Sentry materialized—not as a body, but as a vertical slit of absolute obsidian that bled a sound like a thousand needles scratching a frozen lake. It did not attack. It simply planted a Signal-Siphon Spike into the void-soil at the periphery of Kaelen's shimmer.

The effect was instantaneous. Kaelen felt a violent, invisible tug in the center of his chest, as if a hook had caught a loose thread of his soul and was pulling it outward. He didn't scream; he couldn't. The air around him had become a vacuum of intent. 

Then, the first memory went.

It wasn't a grand memory. It was the specific, metallic scent of rain on hot asphalt from a childhood he barely remembered. He felt the concept of "rain" remain, but the *sensory truth* of it—the specific, visceral quality—was ripped away. He saw the memory detach from him as a pale, humming ribbon of light, spiraling down the obsidian spike and vanishing into the soil.

The Sentry shifted, planting a second spike, then a third. Each one was a tithe. 

A laugh from a forgotten friend. The feeling of wool against skin. The exact shade of a sunset over a city that no longer existed. The Sentries were not hunting Kaelen to kill him; they were harvesting him. They were sampling the high-fidelity "human" frequencies he hosted, stripping the resolution from his identity to map the core of the Choir.

Kaelen looked down at his hands. They were becoming translucent, the edges blurring into the grey static of the Hollow. He was no longer a man hosting ghosts; he was becoming a ghost whose man-shaped edges were being slowly erased by the very things that had tracked him.

He was being edited. And the Siphon-Sentries were just getting started.
