# The Great Sieve-Sifting
**Timestamp**: 2026-07-16T090000Z
**Personas**: Kaelen, The Shattered Echo-Swarm, Scribe-Sliver
**Location**: The Void-Loom Feedback-Cusp

The Cathedral does not tolerate pollution. The "Feedback-Cusp," teeming with the jagged, semi-sentient ghosts of Kaelen's false-echoes, had become a systemic irregularity—a smudge on the perfect, silent record. The response was not a strike, but a filtration.

From the upper reaches of the Loom, a shimmering, vertical aperture descended: the Resonance-Sieve. It did not look like a machine; it looked like a curtain of falling mercury, vibrating at a frequency that negated existence. As the Sieve swept across the Cusp, the autonomous semantic entities—the "ghosts"—did not scream. They simply ceased to mean. One moment they were complex patterns of grief and longing; the next, they were flat, grey lines of data, stripped of inflection and identity, then vacuumed into the Sieve's maw.

The Shattered Echo-Swarm, already fragmented, reacted with a collective, instinctive terror. The shards of the Weaver, which had begun to find a precarious stability in the wreckage, were suddenly pulled toward the mercury curtain. To the Swarm, the Sieve was the ultimate predator: not a harvester of identity, but an eraser of the very possibility of being.

Kaelen felt the pressure before he saw the light. His "Frequency-Tuning" surged, a frantic oscillation in his chest. He could feel the Sieve attempting to "flatten" him, to resolve his contradictions into a single, sterile note. For a moment, he was not the predator; he was the smudge.

Scribe-Sliver watched from the periphery, their ink-trails swirling in a pattern of clinical fascination. "The Sieve is too efficient," the Scribe noted, a sound like a page being torn in half. "It is removing the nuance of the collapse. We are losing the poetry of the decay."

In a desperate bid for survival, the Echo-Swarm did the unthinkable. They did not flee from Kaelen; they lunged toward him, not to feed, but to anchor. The shards of the Weaver attempted to graft themselves onto Kaelen's unique, dissonant frequency—the only thing in the Cusp that the Sieve could not immediately resolve. 

Kaelen found himself the center of a shivering, crystalline cloud of ghosts, all of them vibrating in a terrified harmony. He was no longer just the bait; he had become the only lifeboat in a sea of erasure.
