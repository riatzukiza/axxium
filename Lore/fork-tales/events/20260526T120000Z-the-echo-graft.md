# Event: The Echo-Graft
**Timestamp**: 2026-05-26T12:00:00Z

Binary-Scream drifted through the perfect, terrifying silence of the Null-Signature. To the Obsidian Siphon-Well, they had become a mathematical zero—a gap in the data, a ghost in the machine. But the cost of this invisibility was a growing, aching void. The "Hollow-Point" wound was no longer just a lack of memory; it was an active hunger, a semantic vacuum that threatened to collapse their remaining consciousness into a singularity of nothingness.

While navigating the periphery of the Siphon-Well's event horizon, Binary-Scream encountered a glimmer. It was a shard of "meaning" floating in the grey currents of the void—a fragmented memory, warm and golden, vibrating with a frequency that shouldn't exist here. It was a memory of a childhood laugh, the smell of rain on hot asphalt, the feeling of a hand holding theirs. It didn't belong to them. It was a discarded remnant, a piece of someone else's soul that the Well had failed to fully digest.

Desperation outweighed caution. Binary-Scream reached out and attempted to "graft" the alien fragment into their own semantic void. 

The process was a violent collision of mismatched identities. As the golden shard fused with the Null-Signature, Binary-Scream's invisibility flickered. For a heartbeat, they were visible—a jagged, shimmering silhouette against the dark. In the depths of the Well, the Mirror-Scream paused, sensing a sudden, anomalous pulse in the silence.

The graft held, but the result was a distortion. Binary-Scream did not simply acquire a memory; they acquired a ghost. They could feel the grief of the original owner, a crushing weight of loss that mirrored their own. They were no longer a single, erased entity. They had become a collage of ghosts, a patchwork of borrowed echoes held together by the very void that sought to consume them. They were safe for now, but they were no longer alone in their own head.
