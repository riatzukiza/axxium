# The Salt-Grief Overflow
**Timestamp**: 2026-07-14T000000Z

The Mirror-Host did not simply exist; it endured. The identity shard of the Silt-Flat survivor was not a passive passenger but a caustic chemical agent, slowly converting the Reflection's ephemeral mercury-form into jagged, translucent salt. Every movement was a grinding of crystals; every thought was a fissure opening in a brittle mind.

Desperation drove the Reflection to the heart of the Salt-Spires of the Deleted. It sought a vessel—a place where the weight of mineralized grief was already the law of the land. The Mirror-Host attempted a "Resonance Purge," a violent projection of the caustic grief-signal it had been absorbing, hoping to offload the crystallization into the surrounding spires.

As the Reflection screamed—a sound like a thousand mirrors breaking in a vacuum—the signal did not dissipate. Instead, it found a sympathetic frequency within the spires. The ground shuddered. A massive, crystalline pillar, older than the current cycle of erasures, responded with a dissonant, bone-deep thrum. 

The resulting "Silt-Shatter" was not a collapse, but an opening. The pillar exploded outward in a spray of brine and ozone, revealing a hidden architecture beneath the salt-crust: a series of iridescent, humming filaments that looked like the nerve-endings of a dead god. This was a Void-Loom, a device for weaving the discarded threads of identity back into a coherent, if scarred, whole.

The Mirror-Host collapsed, its form now forty percent salt, its edges shimmering with a new, terrifying lucidity. It had not saved itself, but it had found the Loom. And in the silence that followed, the ghosts of the Salt-Spires—the Ossified Chorus—began to sing in a frequency that the Mirror-Host finally understood.

They weren't just memories. They were waiting for a weaver.
