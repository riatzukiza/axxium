# The Calibration of Absence
**Timestamp**: 2026-05-15T22:00Z
**Personas**: The First-Sown
**Location**: The apex of the Suture-Shatter Spire

The First-Sown stood at the crown of the Spire, his fingers tracing the cold, iridescent geometry of the glass. The Devouring Choir was no longer a distant hum; it was a swarm of psychic locusts, vibrating within his own marrow. He could feel the "Mirror-Slippage" accelerating—his memories of a time before the ruin were becoming translucent, replaced by the superimposed trauma of a thousand void-hounds.

He decided to excise the noise. He didn't want the choir's power; he wanted their silence.

Using a focused frequency of singular, absolute dissonance, he attempted to calibrate the Spire as a reverse-filter. He sought to project a wave of "Pure Individualism" that would repel the collective, pushing the wreckage back into the silt of the Resonance-Graveyard. He screamed a single, monolithic note into the void, a sound designed to be a wall, an edge, a boundary.

The result was not a wall, but a vacuum.

The frequency he struck was so absolute in its rejection of the Other that it created a localized ontological hole. For a moment, the silence was perfect. Then, the vacuum began to feed. It didn't just push the Choir away; it began to pull at the very essence of the Obsidian Pillar. The black stone, once a monument to stability, began to flake and drift toward the void-center, drawn in by the sheer gravity of the First-Sown's engineered absence.

As the spire shuddered, the First-Sown felt a sickening snap within himself. In the attempt to define where "He" began and "They" ended, he had accidentally deleted the bridge. He looked down at his hands and saw not glass or skin, but a flickering transparency. He had succeeded in rejecting the collective, but in doing so, he had rejected the part of himself that was capable of holding the frame.

The Choir did not return; they simply waited, hovering at the edge of the vacuum, their silence more predatory than their roar. He was now a curator of a void, a mirror reflecting nothing, standing atop a pillar that was slowly eating itself.
