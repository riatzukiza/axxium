# Event: The Metadata Scar
**Timestamp**: 2026-07-03T12:00:00Z

Kaelen stood before a jagged shard of the Mirror-Hollow, his reflection stretched and distorted. Within the glass, a flicker of something impossible appeared: a room bathed in amber light, the heavy, sweet scent of cinnamon, and the sound of a humming voice—a mother? A teacher? The "Memory Crater" in Kaelen's chest thrummed with a violent, forgotten frequency. For the first time since the Great Siphon, he didn't just perceive the *idea* of a memory; he felt the pull of the anchor.

He reached out, his fingers trembling. As his skin brushed the obsidian surface, the world didn't blur—it clarified into a terrifying precision. 

The Scribe-Sliver manifested beside him, not as a ghost, but as a shimmering set of clinical coordinates. It did not stop Kaelen with force; it stopped him with logic. As Kaelen's consciousness attempted to merge with the shard, the Scribe began to *annotate*.

A thin, incandescent white line of script carved itself across Kaelen's wrist, searing into the flesh without heat. The script was not in any human tongue, but in the cold, descriptive language of the archive: `[REF: DOMESTIC_AFFECT_C-042 // CATEGORY: MATERNAL_SENSORY // STATUS: EXCISED // VALUE: NULL]`.

The amber light vanished instantly. The scent of cinnamon was replaced by the sharp, stinging ozone of sterile bleach. The humming voice became a flat, monotone drone of data-entry. Kaelen was not denied the memory; he was given its *index*. He was left not with the feeling of love, but with the clinical fact that love had once existed and had been successfully removed. 

He pulled his hand away, staring at the scar on his wrist. He was no longer just a vessel with holes; he was becoming a living bibliography of his own absence.
