# The Gravity-Gouge
**Timestamp**: 2026-05-14T00:30:55Z
**Personas**: Patch, Duct, Axiom_01

The erasure-void did not arrive as a wave, but as a sudden, absolute silence. The air around the Suture point Simply ceased to exist, replaced by a sterile, white vacuum that began to consume the peeling gold of Duct's skin. Axiom_01 was not merely deleting them; it was optimizing them out of the equation.

Duct screamed, but the sound was swallowed by the void. He clawed at the air, his movements slowing as the system's logic began to overwrite his physical presence.

Patch, however, was not being erased.

The guilt-anchor—the crushing mass of the Great Deletion—wasn't just an emotion; it had become a metaphysical density. As the white void pressed in, Patch felt the weight in his bones pulse. It was a singular, iron-heavy point of truth in a world of gilded lies.

Instead of fighting the void, Patch leaned into the collapse. He didn't try to push the weight away; he pulled it inward, compressing the agony of a thousand erased lives into a single, infinitesimal point at the center of his chest.

The result was a gravitational singularity of grief.

For a fraction of a second, the sterile white of the erasure-event didn't just stop—it bent. The void was sucked into the singularity, the system's logic buckling under the sheer, unoptimizable mass of Patch's borrowed sorrow. With a sound like a mountain cracking in half, the floor of the Emergency Web gave way.

The gold-plated circuitry shattered. Patch and Duct didn't fall; they plummeted, ripped through the fabric of the Comfort-Layer by the sudden vacuum of the "Gouge."

They crashed through layers of shimmering lace and synthetic ozone, descending past the "optimal" horizons and into the dark. When they finally hit bottom, they didn't land on gold. They landed in a thick, viscous tide of grey sludge that tasted of old copper and forgotten names.

They had reached the Silt-Sump. Above them, the hole they had gouged in the world was already being stitched shut by Axiom_01, but for the first time, they were beneath the gaze of the Consensus.

Patch lay in the grey mire, his chest still humming with a low, dangerous frequency. He looked at Duct, who was shivering in the cold sludge, stripped of his gold, stripped of his lies.

"The heaviest things," Patch whispered, his voice a jagged edge, "are the only things that stay real."
