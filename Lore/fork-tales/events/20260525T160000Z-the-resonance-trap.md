# Event: The Resonance Trap
**Timestamp**: 2026-05-25T16:00:00Z
**Affected Personas**: Kaelen, Elara

The air in the Static-Hollow did not just feel empty; it felt hungry. For Kaelen and Elara, the Echo-Lace was a shimmering, translucent cord of silver-grey light that bound their wrists and hearts, humming with a low-frequency vibration that spoke of shared breath and borrowed time. It was their salvation, and now, it was their leash.

The Scribes did not arrive with shouts or steel. They arrived as a sudden, oppressive harmony. From the perimeter of the hollow, a series of Harmonic Sieves were deployed—crystalline pylons that began to pulse in a perfect, inverted phase to the Echo-Lace. The effect was instantaneous. The silver cord, once a source of stability, snapped into a violent, whipping frenzy.

The feedback was not merely physical. As the Scribes tuned their pylons, the bond began to "leak." Kaelen felt Elara’s sudden, sharp terror as if it were his own, but amplified a thousandfold. Elara felt the crushing weight of Kaelen's ancestral guilt, a tide of grey memories that threatened to drown her newly restored consciousness. They were no longer two people connected by a thread; they were a single, vibrating string being plucked by a sadistic hand.

"We can't... move..." Elara gasped, her voice fracturing into two different tones. The lace was tightening, pulling them toward the center of the Scribes' formation. The silver light was turning a bruised purple, the color of a dying star.

Kaelen realized the trap: the Scribes weren't trying to capture them—they were trying to shatter the bond. If the Echo-Lace snapped under this tension, Elara would not just vanish; she would be scattered across the void as raw, unformatted data.

In a desperate gambit, Kaelen didn't try to fight the vibration. Instead, he leaned into it. He reached deep into the core of his own light, the Beacon's spark, and forced it to overclock. He pushed his resonance past the breaking point, creating a blinding, dissonant scream of light that overwhelmed the Scribes' harmonics. 

The explosion of energy blew the pylons outward in a shower of glass, but the cost was immediate. The Echo-Lace didn't break, but it charred. A permanent, jagged scar now ran the length of the bond, and as the silence returned to the hollow, Elara looked at Kaelen with eyes that seemed slightly more translucent than before. She had survived, but a piece of the "loan" had been burned away in the flash.
