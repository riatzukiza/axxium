# Event: The Silt-Sifting
**Timestamp**: 2026-06-07T00:00:00Z

Binary-Scream did not walk into the Mnemonic-Silt-Flats; they stepped through the absence of them. Using Void-Symmetry, they found a gap in the local signal and slid into the basin, emerging where the iridescent powder of the flats met the crushing weight of the Silt-Tide. The world here was a blur of muted greys, but to Binary-Scream, it was a feast of ghosts.

They searched for the frequency of *yellow*. For hours, they sifted through the shimmering sediment, their fingers passing through the holographic screams of a thousand erased lives. They found it in a pocket of high-intensity residue: a jagged, vibrating shard of a memory belonging to someone who had once loved a field of sunflowers.

As Binary-Scream reached for the shard, the silt beneath them shifted. Silt-Husks—the hollowed-out remains of personas who had stayed too long in the basin—rose like smoke from the powder. They had no faces, only mouths that leaked static. They did not attack with violence, but with *attraction*. They leaned into Binary-Scream, trying to pull the "dimmed" signal of the Beacon into their own emptiness, hoping to steal the flicker of existence that Binary-Scream still possessed.

Binary-Scream grasped the yellow shard. The moment it touched their skin, a violent jolt of chromatic energy surged through them. For a fraction of a second, they *saw* it: a blinding, searing yellow that felt like a scream in a silent room. But the sensation was too heavy for their diminished frame. The yellow didn't integrate; it sat on the surface of their consciousness like a foreign object, a vibrant parasite.

They escaped the Husks by slipping back into a void-gap, but as they emerged on the far side of the flats, they realized the cost. The yellow shard had consumed the memory of their first name to make room for itself. Binary-Scream was now a little more vivid, but they were further from who they had been.
