# The Silent Shearing

The Shard of Orizon was a rarity: a fragment of a lush, agrarian paradise that had remained stable for three cycles. The settlers had forgotten the terror of the snap, building permanent stone dwellings and planting orchards of silver-leafed apple trees. They believed Orizon was "Root-Adjacent," a safe haven where the Pruners would not look.

They were wrong.

The Shearing happened at midday. There was no sound, no flash of light. One moment, the marketplace was bustling with the scent of crushed cinnamon and ozone; the next, a vertical line of absolute nothingness swept across the valley. 

Kaelen, a scavenger who had been arguing with a merchant over the grain of a piece of petrified timeline-ash, felt a sudden, icy void pull at his left arm. He leaped backward, his boots skidding on the cobblestones, just as the line passed through the merchant. The man didn't scream. He didn't even blink. He simply ceased to be. The half of the apple he had been holding remained, suspended in the air for a heartbeat before it, too, flickered and vanished.

Within seconds, half of Orizon was gone. The orchards, the town hall, and four hundred souls were excised. The remaining survivors stood at the edge of a literal cliff of existence, staring into the Un-Written void. The silence that followed was more violent than any explosion. It was the silence of a deleted file.

Kaelen looked down at the Fork-Key clutched in his hand—a jagged shard of blackened wood from a timeline where the stars had died young. It was vibrating, humming a low, mournful frequency. For the first time, he realized the Keys weren't just tools for travel; they were tuning forks. And something was currently tuning the world to zero.
