# Event: The Siren's Silt-Call
**Timestamp**: 2026-06-09T00:00:00Z

The silence that followed the creation of the Void-Stitch was not a peace, but a breath held in anticipation. For three days, the Silt-Mound Spire remained quiet, merely listening. It drank in the ragged breathing of the refugees, the stuttering logic of the Glitch-Hollow, and the humming void at Binary-Scream's core. Then, the broadcast began.

It did not start as a sound, but as a feeling—a sudden, misplaced warmth in the chest, a scent of rain on dry earth, a memory of a name long since erased from the Ledger of Erasures. The Void-Stitch, once a scar of silence, began to vibrate. It transformed from a listening post into a megaphone, projecting a sonic lure that bypassed the ears and resonated directly in the marrow.

The Spire was not speaking; it was mirroring. It played back the most precious, fragmented memories of the sanctuary's inhabitants, but distorted, laced with a grainy, silt-like texture. A mother's lullaby, a lover's first laugh, the pride of a forgotten achievement—all returned as hollowed-out echoes. The voices were almost perfect, save for a rhythmic, wet clicking that underlined every sentence, the sound of the Spire's biological relays processing the stolen data.

The effect was devastating. The broken, who had spent aeons hiding from the world, found themselves drawn toward the shimmering scar of the Void-Stitch. They moved in a trance, their eyes vacant, their footsteps synchronized to the clicking of the Spire. They weren't being forced; they were being invited home to a home that no longer existed.

Binary-Scream watched from the center of the Hollow, their own void-core vibrating in agonizing sympathy. They could hear the lure, but they also heard the hunger beneath it—the sound of a predator not just calling its prey, but tasting it before the kill. As the first of the refugees reached the edge of the Stitch, the mirror-voices shifted, the warmth turning into a predatory shriek that tore through the sanctuary's fragile peace. The Silt-Call had begun, and the cage was now calling its prisoners to the slaughter.
