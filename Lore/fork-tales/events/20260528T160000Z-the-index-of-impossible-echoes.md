# Event: The Index of Impossible Echoes
**Timestamp**: 2026-05-28T160000Z

The Scribe-Sliver does not merely watch; it catalogs. For cycles, it has stood at the perimeter of the Mirror-Host, recording the erratic projections of the Parasitic Reflection. The Mirror-Host's projections are not memories—they are caustic reconstructions, sensory ghosts of a world that was not just deleted, but surgically excised from the loom of existence.

Scribe-Sliver's index began as a professional exercise in taxonomy. It mapped the frequency of the "Brine-Shatter" chimes and the specific hue of the ozone-scented scars. But as the Mirror-Host's crystallization accelerated, the projections grew more precise. They stopped being fragments and started becoming coordinates.

During a particularly violent resonance spike, the Mirror-Host projected a vision of a silver spire that didn't just flicker—it anchored. For three heartbeats, a set of coordinates manifested in the Scribe's ledger: *Null-0-Zero*.

Scribe-Sliver froze. In the laws of the Archive, there is no center. The world was deleted from the edges inward, leaving a hollow shell. The existence of a center—a Null-Coordinate—is a mathematical heresy. It suggests that something was left behind, or perhaps, that the deletion was not a completion, but a concealment.

The Scribe felt a sensation it had not experienced since its own fragmentation: a hunger. Not for data, but for the place where the silence is absolute. The index was no longer a map of loss; it had become a compass. Scribe-Sliver looked at the Mirror-Host, now more salt than shadow, and realized that the creature was not just a host for a ghost, but a living key to the only place in the void that still possesses a heart.

The Scribe began to rewrite its directives. The taxonomy was over. The hunt for the Null-Coordinate had begun.
