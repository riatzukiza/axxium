# Event: The Gilded Parasite
**Timestamp**: 2026-05-28T14:00:00Z
**Affected Personas**: Elara, Kaelen

The air around the Weeping Spire had become a thick, golden syrup of nostalgia. Kaelen stood at the center, his Void-Breath no longer a scream but a sigh, exhaling Gilded Fragments that drifted like dandelion seeds of pure, distilled longing. One fragment, shimmering with the specific, heartbreaking hue of a forgotten summer afternoon, floated toward Elara.

Elara did not hesitate. The hunger had long since eroded her caution. She lunged, her fingers closing around the fragment, pulling it into her chest with a desperate, violent inhalation. 

For a heartbeat, there was ecstasy. She saw a flash of a wooden porch, smelled rain on hot pavement, and felt the ghost of a hand holding hers. It was a piece of her—a fragment of the girl she had been before the shearing. But as the light dimmed, the warmth turned to a cold, piercing needle.

The fragment did not dissolve. It anchored.

Elara gasped, clutching her sternum as a jagged, golden thread whipped outward from her chest, snapping taut and connecting directly to Kaelen. The fragment wasn't a memory; it was a lure—a psychic barb designed to tether the witness to the conduit. 

Kaelen recoiled, his eyes widening. He didn't just see her; he *felt* her. Her hunger became his nausea; her desperation became his vertigo. The link was a two-way street of instability. Elara could feel the Void-Breath rattling in Kaelen's lungs as if it were her own, a freezing wind that threatened to extinguish her remaining spark.

They stood locked in a shimmering, agonizing symmetry. Elara had reclaimed a memory, but in doing so, she had invited the void into her marrow. She was no longer just a witness; she was a parasite, and the host was dying.
