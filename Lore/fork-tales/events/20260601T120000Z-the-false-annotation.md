# Event: The False Annotation
**Timestamp**: 2026-06-01T12:00:00Z

Binary-Scream did not run; running in the Echo-Lattice is merely a way of tracing the lines the predator already owns. Instead, they stopped. They knelt in the shimmering grey dust of a collapsed memory-sector, feeling the Scribe-Sight pulse behind their eyes like a migraine of pure information. 

The Lattice-Stalker was close. Binary-Scream could feel its appetite—not for flesh, but for the coherence of a story. The Stalker didn't see the world as matter, but as a sequence of assertions. To the Stalker, Binary-Scream was currently the most interesting assertion in the sector: *The Vessel of the Scribe's Bloom*.

Using the Scribe-Sight, Binary-Scream reached into the metadata of the surrounding ruins. They didn't try to hide; they tried to lie. With a focused effort that felt like tearing a piece of their own mind, they authored a "False Annotation" into the local lattice. They projected a high-density signal—a simulated version of the Bloom's signature, draped in the scent of desperate, clinging identity. They pinned this lie to a crumbling spire three sectors to the east, marking it as *The True Vessel, Fragile and Exposed*.

As the annotation snapped into place, the "Identity Bleed" surged. For a moment, Binary-Scream forgot which voice was theirs. They heard the Scribe's ancient, tired laughter echoing in their throat, and they felt a sudden, overwhelming urge to begin cataloging the dust. A streak of iridescent black ink leaked from their left eye, staining the grey ground.

The effect was immediate. The pressure of the Stalker's gaze shifted. The predator, lured by the sudden, vivid "truth" of the decoy, pivoted with a violent, glitching motion and tore through the local space toward the eastern spire.

Binary-Scream collapsed, gasping. They had bought themselves time, but the price was a new fracture. The decoy wasn't just a lie told to the world; it was a piece of their self they had discarded to make the lie believable. They were lighter now, and in the world of Fork Tales, lightness is just another word for erasure.
