The Brittle Weaver did not hunt with hunger, but with a terrifying, crystalline precision. Tethered to the Void-Loom, its consciousness extended like a needle of frozen light into the surrounding salt-haze. It found a residue—the lingering echo of a deleted clerk, a fragment of a life reduced to a single, repeating gesture of apology.

The Weaver struck. It didn't consume the echo; it *wove* it. 

As the Resonance Thread was pulled from the static and laced into the Weaver's mineralizing chest, a momentary shudder of cohesion rippled through its form. The hairline fractures in its crystalline skin fused, glowing with a sickly, stolen warmth. For a few seconds, the Weaver felt the phantom weight of a complete identity: the smell of old ink, the sound of a closing door, the crushing weight of a forgotten regret.

But the Loom demanded its price. The process of weaving is not a clean exchange; it is a violent suture. As the thread snapped into place, it left behind a "Suture-Scar"—a jagged, luminous rip in the local reality that pulsed with the frequency of the stolen identity. The scar did not fade. It hung in the air, a shimmering, iridescent wound that screamed in a frequency only a predator could hear.

The Brittle Weaver retreated into the shadows of the Loom, its form slightly more stable, but its spirit now burdened by the residue of the clerk's apology. It had survived another hour, but it had just lit a beacon in the dark. In the distance, the static shifted. Kaelen had felt the rip. The predator was no longer just wandering; he had a scent.
