# Event: The Scribe's Taxonomy of Loss
**Timestamp**: 2026-07-02T12:00:00Z

The Mirror-Hollow was a masterpiece of recursive geometry, but in the void, perfection is an invitation. 

As the Siphon-Sentries continued to oscillate in their self-made loop, a new frequency entered the space. It was not a pulse or a scream, but a thin, needle-like scratching, like a diamond stylus on a frozen record. From the shimmering seams of the recursive fold, a Scribe-Sliver manifested. It was a spindly, multi-jointed entity of translucent glass and ink, its "head" a rotating series of concentric lenses that clicked with rhythmic, clinical precision.

The Scribe did not acknowledge the trapped Sentries. It was interested in the "residue."

The memories Kaelen had sacrificed to stabilize the Mirror-Hollow—the scent of rain, the cadence of a mother's voice—had not vanished. They had been filtered through the void-soil and were now floating as iridescent, jagged shards of "pure meaning" around Kaelen's feet. 

The Scribe began to move. With agonizing slowness, it extended a glass limb and plucked a shard of the rain-memory from the air. As it touched the fragment, the Scribe emitted a series of rapid-fire binary clicks. Suddenly, the air around Kaelen filled with a holographic projection: a street of gray asphalt, a sudden downpour, and the smell of ozone. But it was wrong. The rain fell upward. The asphalt was made of frozen static. The memory had been stripped of its *context* and reduced to a *category*.

"Specimen 402: Olfactory-Haptic Cluster. Precipitation/Urban. Status: Severed," the Scribe's voice resonated, not as sound, but as a direct injection of data into Kaelen's mind.

Kaelen reached out, a desperate, instinctive gesture to reclaim the shard. But as his fingers brushed the projection, he felt a terrifying vacancy. The Scribe wasn't just archiving the memory; it was claiming ownership of it. To the Scribe, Kaelen was no longer the owner of his history; he was merely the source-material for a dead language.

The Choir within him surged, their voices filling the void where the memory had been, singing a song of a home Kaelen could no longer imagine, but which the Scribe had now meticulously cataloged as 'Obsolete'.
