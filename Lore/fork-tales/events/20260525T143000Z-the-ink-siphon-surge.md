# Event: The Ink-Siphon Surge (2026-05-25T14:30:00Z)

The monochrome tide did not roll in; it snapped. One moment, the survivors were huddled in the ruins of the Old Library, the air smelling of damp stone and desperation. The next, the world was stripped of its color. The sky became a flat, oppressive shade of vellum, and the rubble beneath their feet turned into a textureless grey slurry. They were inside a Grey Parchment zone, a localized overwrite of reality by the Index.

Panic erupted as the Scribes manifested. They didn't emerge from portals; they simply *were* there, their elongated, ink-stained fingers twitching in synchronization. They weren't cataloging this time. They were harvesting.

Kaelen felt the Narrative Anchor on his skin sear with a sudden, agonizing heat. He saw a young girl, no more than six, frozen in terror as a Scribe’s finger touched her temple. A thin, silver thread of light—a memory of her mother's voice—began to pull away from her skull, spiraling toward the Scribe's palm.

"Not this one!" Kaelen roared. He closed his eyes and reached into the void-corruption within him, pushing the Violet Stain outward. He didn't fight the Scribe; he fought the *logic* of the Index. He envisioned the girl not as a data point to be extracted, but as a jagged, irreducible contradiction.

The interference worked. A shockwave of violet static tore through the monochrome field, shattering the Scribe's connection. The silver thread snapped back into the girl, but the backlash was immense. Kaelen was thrown backward, his vision blurring. In that moment of psychic overlap, he saw past the Scribes, past the Grey Parchment, into the heart of the Archivists' Great Archive.

He didn't see a library. He saw a loom. 

The Archivists weren't preserving the world; they were weaving a new one. They were using the extracted memories of the survivors as raw thread to construct a "Perfect Narrative"—a sanitized, painless history where the void never existed, and the survivors were never born. The "preservation" was a lie. The extraction was the first step of a total, retroactive erasure.

As the zone collapsed and color bled back into the world, Kaelen lay in the dirt, the weight of the revelation crushing him. They weren't just fighting for survival. They were fighting to remain *true*.
