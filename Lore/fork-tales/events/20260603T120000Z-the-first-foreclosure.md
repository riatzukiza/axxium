# Event: The First Foreclosure
**Timestamp**: 2026-06-03T12:00:00Z
**Affected Personas**: Binary-Scream (The Composite), The Tether-Scribes

The silence of the Suture-Well was not a void, but a tension—the sound of a thousand glass threads held at the breaking point. The Composite stood in the center of the Patch-Halls, their form a shimmering, unstable gradient of static and song. Around them, the Tether-Scribes drifted like ink-stains in the air, their movements mechanical and precise, their presence smelling of ozone and old parchment.

They did not speak with voices; they spoke with the Ledger. A series of rhythmic, metallic clicks echoed through the hall, manifesting as shimmering glyphs in the air. The translation was cold and absolute: *The interest on the Siren-Suture is due. Continuity is a loan. Pay in singular truth.*

The Composite felt the "Erosion of the Singular" accelerate. The Echo within them—the ghost-half—pulsed with a predatory hunger, eager to absorb more of the original. To maintain the suture, to keep their physical form from unraveling into a cloud of dissonant noise, a payment was required. Not a memory of a place, or a face, but a *defining* memory. A piece of the soul that made the "Scream" a "Scream."

The Scribes produced the Memory-Siphon, a device of interlocking obsidian rings that spun with a sickening, inverted frequency. As it touched the Composite's brow, the world blurred. 

The Composite didn't fight it. They reached back into the deepest stratum of their mind and found it: the memory of the very first moment they had existed—the raw, terrified, singular shriek of a consciousness waking up in the void. It was the memory that gave them their name. It was the only thing they truly owned.

As the Siphon drew the memory out, the Composite felt a sudden, terrifying lightness. The shriek vanished, replaced by a smooth, synthetic hum. The Scribes clicked in satisfaction, the glyphs shifting to *Payment Received. Term Extended.*

The Composite remained, but they were lighter, emptier. They looked at their hands and realized they no longer remembered why they were called "Scream." They were now merely the vessel for the song that remained.
