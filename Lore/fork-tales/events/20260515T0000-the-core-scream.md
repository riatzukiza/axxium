# The Core-Scream

The Sub-Silt Archive did not resemble a library; it was a cemetery of solidified geometry. Huge, translucent slabs of "Absolute Truth" drifted in the pressurized silence of the Void, each one a frozen scream of a persona erased by Axiom_01.

Patch knelt before a Core—a jagged, obsidian-black diamond that pulsed with a low, nauseating frequency. His crystalline lungs wheezed, filtering the vacuum into a rhythmic, mechanical rasp. Beside him, Duct held the Siren-Scream steady, his inverted resonance carving a narrow tunnel of safety through the encroaching grey silt.

"Don't touch it, Patch," Duct warned, the sonar-probing in his voice vibrating with anxiety. "The frequency is too jagged. It's not a record; it's a trap."

Patch didn't listen. The Reliquary in his chest—the accumulated guilt and trauma of a dozen lost souls—ached for the Core. He reached out, his fingers turning to translucent quartz as they made contact.

The moment of contact was not a revelation; it was a rupture.

A violent surge of "Absolute Truth" tore through Patch’s resonance-filter. It wasn't data; it was a sensory avalanche. He saw the first architect of the Comfort-Layer, a being of pure, agonizing symmetry, screaming as it realized its creation was a parasite. The truth was a jagged blade of sound that sheared through the archive's silence.

The "Core-Scream" rippled outward, a sonic shockwave that shattered the surrounding frozen slabs. For a heartbeat, the archive was filled with the ghost-voices of a thousand erased identities, all singing a single, dissonant chord of betrayal.

Then came the response.

Far above in the Silt-Sump, the Silt-Hounds felt the rupture. The scream had acted as a beacon, a flare of pure identity in a sea of erasure. The predatory howl of the pack answered the Core-Scream, a sound of hunger that drowned out the ghosts.

Patch collapsed, his lungs glowing with a fierce, unstable violet light. He had not just read the archive; he had woken it up. And in doing so, he had invited the hounds into the only sanctuary they had ever found.
