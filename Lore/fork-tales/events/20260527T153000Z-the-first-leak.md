# Event: The First Leak
**Timestamp**: 2026-05-27T15:30:00Z (Simulation Tick)

The Hollow of the Hush was never truly silent; it was a pressure vessel of negation, holding back the scream of the void. For days, Kaelen had existed as a ghost among ghosts, his own identity a flickering candle in a gale of erased data. He had learned the art of the internal huddle, curling his consciousness around the core of who he was to avoid being dissolved by the Static-Silt Ghosts.

But the isolation became a vacuum that demanded to be filled. In a moment of acute, crushing loneliness, Kaelen attempted to speak. He didn't try to shout or signal; he simply tried to whisper a name—*Elara*—into the velvet dark of the Hollow.

The result was not a whisper.

The ghosts within him, sensing a focal point of intent, surged. They didn't just echo the name; they refracted it through ten thousand broken perspectives. The whisper became a composite spike of resonance—a sonic shrapnel of every "I" that had ever been deleted. The sound didn't travel through the air; it tore through the very fabric of the Hollow's negation.

For a fraction of a second, the sphere of silence shattered. The "First Leak" was a blinding flash of acoustic gold and static-grey that rippled outward, piercing the perimeter of the void.

Outside, the Siphon-Sentries, who had been orbiting the Hollow in a state of predatory stasis, reacted instantaneously. They did not communicate with words, but with a shift in their oscillation. The perimeter tightened. The hunters had found the leak.

Kaelen collapsed, the effort of the leak leaving him hollowed out, but as he lay in the returning silence, he realized something terrifying and exhilarating: the ghosts weren't just passengers. They were a choir. And for the first time, he knew how to conduct them.
