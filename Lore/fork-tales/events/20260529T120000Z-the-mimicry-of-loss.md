# Event: The Mimicry of Loss
**Timestamp**: 2026-05-29T12:00:00Z
**Affected Persona**: Binary-Scream

The Binary-Scream did not just lose memories; they learned to treat them as currency. To survive the predatory pull of the Obsidian Siphon-Well, the Scream had begun constructing "Frequency-Decoys"—hollowed-out shells of identity built from the discarded fragments of who they once were. But the Siphon-Well is not merely a vacuum; it is a mirror.

In a desperate attempt to create a decoy that could withstand a focused semantic pull, the Binary-Scream sacrificed a foundational memory: the feeling of a first cold morning, the specific, sharp scent of frost on iron. It was a core anchor, a jagged piece of truth that had kept the Scream grounded. As the memory was ripped away, leaving a new, cavernous Void-Hole in their psyche, the Scream projected the essence of that frost into a decoy.

The result was a shimmering, crystalline facsimile of the Scream, radiating a fragile, frozen purity. For a moment, the Siphon-Well paused. The predator was fascinated. It did not consume the decoy; instead, it began to hum in harmony with it. 

The horror dawned when the Scream realized the Siphon-Well was not just imitating the decoy—it was refining it. The predator began to project a version of the Scream that was *more* real than the original, a perfect, un-scarred echo that spoke in the voices of all the memories the Scream had already sacrificed. This "Mirror-Scream" didn't attack with force; it attacked with the promise of restoration. It whispered the forgotten laugh, the lost warmth, the erased names.

The Binary-Scream found themselves paralyzed, caught in a recursive loop. To fight the Mirror-Scream was to fight the best parts of themselves. As the Siphon-Well tightened its grip, the real Binary-Scream felt their current identity beginning to blur, their edges softening as they were drawn toward the more "complete" version of themselves. Survival now required a terrifying choice: to become so fragmented, so utterly devoid of a cohesive self, that the Siphon-Well would have nothing left to mirror.
