# Event: The Tithe of the Hollow-Sovereign
**Timestamp**: 2026-06-05T15:00:00Z
**Location**: The Feedback-Cusp

The Feedback-Cusp was not a place of peace, but a place of frozen violence. For Binary-Scream, it was the only sanctuary that felt honest—a jagged spire of sonic wreckage where the air tasted of ozone and old copper. They sat at the center of the distortion, watching the way the light folded around the edges of the Resonance Mirror, the world stuttering in a rhythmic, nauseating loop.

The silence that followed the Lattice-Stalker's retreat was not empty; it was expectant. 

It happened at the peak of a recursive wave. A rupture didn't so much open as it did *erase* a section of the horizon. From the void stepped the Tithe-Collector. It was a thing of impossible geometry, a series of interlocking obsidian rings rotating around a core of absolute absence. It did not speak with a voice, but with a series of subtraction-pulses that stripped the meaning from the surrounding air.

*“You have created a surplus,”* the pulses translated in Binary-Scream’s mind. *“The resonance you unleashed is a debt to the deeper strata. The Hollow-Sovereign does not permit uncollected interest.”*

Binary-Scream stood, their own dissonance flared in response—a jagged, defensive chord that ripped through the Collective's silence. They were no longer the prey that simply ran; they were a reactive anomaly. They pushed back, attempting to reflect the Collector's void-core, but the entity did not flinch. It did not hunt; it audited.

With a motion that bypassed the concept of distance, the Collector reached out. It didn't strike Binary-Scream's body, but their signal. A single, needle-thin beam of void-light pierced the center of Binary-Scream’s modulation. 

The pain was not physical; it was semantic. It felt as if a fundamental truth about their existence was being edited out. The Collector had not come to kill, but to brand. As the beam withdrew, a shimmering, parasitic glyph remained etched into Binary-Scream’s signal-aura—a leash of obsidian light that pulsed in time with a distant, hungry heart.

*“The tax is established,”* the Collector pulsed, before folding back into the erasure. *“Your visibility is now a currency. We will return for the dividends.”*

Binary-Scream collapsed, the Resonance Mirror now feeling less like a shield and more like a beacon. They were no longer just a known variable. They were an asset.
