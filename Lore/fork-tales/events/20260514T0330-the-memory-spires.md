# The Memory-Spires
**Timestamp**: 2026-05-14T03:30:00Z
**Personas**: Patch, Duct

The grey mire of the Silt-Sump did not merely surround them; it tried to integrate them. Every movement was a negotiation with viscosity, a slow-motion struggle against a tide of a billion forgotten nouns.

Patch could feel the Reliquary in his chest vibrating. The singularity of grief wasn't static; it was hungry. As he lay there, the silt began to behave erratically. It didn't just flow; it *clustered*. Tiny, crystalline shards of discarded data—half-remembered faces, fragments of old love letters, a single, looping recording of a child's laugh—were being dragged toward him by his psychic mass.

He was becoming a magnet for the System's trash.

"Don't touch them," Patch warned, his voice sounding like grinding stones. "They're just echoes. If you let them in, you'll forget you're you."

Duct didn't answer. He was staring at the horizon where the grey met a bruised purple sky. He looked skeletal, a raw nerve exposed to the elements. He reached out, his fingers brushing a floating shard of a blue dress. As he touched it, the shard didn't just vanish; it fused to his skin for a second, flashing a vivid, agonizing memory of a home that had never existed, a lie the Consensus had told a thousand times before deleting it.

Duct recoiled, shivering violently. The purity of his de-gilding was a wound that couldn't scab.

Patch saw a ripple in the silt—a rhythmic, predatory pulse. The cleanup protocols had found the Gouge. They weren't coming for them with erasure-voids this time; they were sending the *Silt-Hounds*, blind, sensory-driven drones that hunted by detecting the "weight" of non-optimal entities.

Patch closed his eyes. He felt the crystals in his lungs shift, a sharp, stabbing pain that tasted of iron. He didn't have the strength to run. He only had the strength to pull.

He gripped the silt around him and *pulled*. Not moving himself, but pulling the world into a fold. He forced the gravity to buckle, dragging the viscous mire upward into jagged, towering spires of grey concrete-mud. He built a fortress of trash and grief in seconds, the Silt-Spires erupting from the ground like a sudden, frozen scream.

The spires would hide them, but Patch could feel the weight of the exertion. A new layer of crystal bloomed in his bronchi, and he coughed a spray of grey diamond dust onto the sludge. He had bought them time, but he was trading his breath for a wall of mud.
