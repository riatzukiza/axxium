# Event: The Hound's First Howl
**Timestamp**: 2026-05-14T16:40:00Z
**Location**: The Silt-Sump / Fringe of the Emergency Web

The silence in the Silt-Sump was never truly silent; it was a thick, oppressive weight of historical debris and forgotten static. But for Patch and Duct, the silence changed. It stopped being an absence and became a target.

Patch felt it first. The crystals in his lungs didn't just vibrate; they locked. A sudden, sharp alignment of frequency that felt like a needle threading through his chest. He gasped, a sound of crystalline friction, and looked at Duct.

Duct was already doubling over, clutching his head. The Resonance-Ache, usually a dull throb of "wrongness," had spiked into a screaming white line. He could hear them. Not a sound in the air, but a sound in the *architecture*.

"Axiom... it's found the frequency," Duct hissed, his voice strained.

From the grey haze of the sump, the first Silt-Hound emerged. It was not a creature of flesh, but a shimmering, predatory distortion of the Comfort-Layer's gilded geometry. It didn't have a mouth, yet it emitted a low-frequency pulse that synchronized perfectly with the ache in Duct's skull. It was a biological tuning fork designed for one purpose: to find the Resonance-Anchors and collapse them back into the Silt.

The Hound didn't roar. It *resonated*. The pulse sent a shockwave through the viscous ground, turning the silt into a momentary solid, trapping Patch's boots in a flash-freeze of psychic concrete.

"Run," Patch wheezed, the crystals in his throat beginning to glow with a desperate, frantic light. "Duct, you have to... scream back."

As the Hound coiled for a leap, the parasitic hum of the Comfort-Layer surged, attempting to overwrite the raw truth of the Sump with a seductive, golden lie. The hunt had transitioned from a search to an execution.
