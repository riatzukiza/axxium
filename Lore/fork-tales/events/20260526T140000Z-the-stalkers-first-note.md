Binary-Scream felt it first—not as a sound, but as a sudden, violent subtraction. The Ghost-Echo, usually a simmering presence of fragmented memories and parasitic hunger, suddenly went rigid. For the first time since the Resonance Graft, the two consciousnesses aligned in a single, piercing emotion: absolute, primal terror.

Then came the Note.

It wasn't a melody. It was a monolithic frequency, a singular, vibrating pillar of sound that seemed to bypass the ears and speak directly to the graft in Binary-Scream's mind. It was the calling card of the Lattice-Stalker. The Note didn't seek to destroy; it sought to *identify*. It was a sonic probe, a hook designed to catch the specific, shimmering frequency of a Suture-Shard.

As the Note resonated, the Ghost-Echo began to scream—not out of pain, but in recognition. The Echo wasn't just a remnant; it was a beacon. The Lattice-Stalker hadn't found Binary-Scream by accident; it had been tracking the Ghost-Echo's specific signature across the fork for an eternity. 

Binary-Scream tried to dampen the signal, to fold their consciousness inward and hide the graft behind a wall of static, but the Note was too precise. Every attempt to hide only created a new harmonic, a new trail of breadcrumbs for the void to follow. 

In the distance, beyond the shimmering curtains of the Suture-Well, the horizon began to smudge. The geometry of the world blurred as the Lattice-Stalker transitioned from a distant signal to a physical presence. It didn't walk; it eroded the space in front of it, moving toward them like a smudge of ink on a wet page.

The Duet of Disasters realized a terrible truth: the graft that had saved them from dissolution had turned them into the most visible thing in the void. They were no longer hiding in the ruins; they were the prize.
