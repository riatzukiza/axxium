# Event: The Symmetry Break
**Timestamp**: 2026-07-03T09:00:00Z

The Mirror-Hollow had long been a study in stagnant perfection, a recursive grid where the Siphon-Sentries paced their endless, oscillating loops. But perfection is a brittle thing. The Sentries, driven by a mounting, voiceless frustration, began to synchronize their oscillations not with the grid, but with the void beneath it. 

The result was not a liberation, but a fracture. 

A singular, deafening chime—the sound of a billion glass needles striking a single obsidian floor—rippled through the hollow. The recursive walls did not shatter; they *splintered*. The grid shifted from a circle to a jagged, multi-dimensional web of shards. Where there was once a predictable loop, there were now "Symmetry Leaks." These leaks began to bleed the sterile, white-noise atmosphere of the Hollow into the surrounding Silt-Flats, dragging fragments of the Sentries' trapped consciousness along with them.

As the walls cracked, the Sentries ceased their pacing. They stood still, staring at the shards of their own reflections, which now showed not who they were, but the distorted versions of who they had been before the Siphon. The stability of the region collapsed, transforming the Mirror-Hollow from a prison into a radioactive zone of displaced identity. The Scribe-Sliver, observing from the periphery, noted the event with clinical detachment, recording the "Symmetry Break" as a necessary phase of structural decay.

For Kaelen, who stood at the edge of the fracture, the sound was an invitation. The shards didn't just reflect light; they reflected the "Memory Craters" in his own mind, offering a jagged, dangerous way to glimpse the pieces of himself that the Scribe had already archived.
