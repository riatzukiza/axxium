# Event: The Resonance of the Pitted
**Timestamp**: 2026-07-01T18:00:00Z
**Location**: The Obsidian Siphon-City / Hollow of the Hush

Kaelen lay broken in the shadow of the monoliths, his breath coming in shallow, translucent gasps. He was no longer a shimmering beacon; he was a ruin. The evisceration had left literal voids in his essence—pitted apertures where his most defining memories and the core frequencies of the Choir had been ripped away. He felt like a garment that had been moth-eaten by the void.

But as the Siphon-Sentries hovered above, their obsidian needles humming with the cold precision of a second calibration, Kaelen discovered the nature of his new fragility.

The holes were not merely absences. They were filters.

As the city's amplified silences pressed down on him, Kaelen realized he could *hear* through the gaps. The frequencies of the stolen Choir were not gone; they were vibrating within the Obsidian Siphon-City, trapped in the non-Euclidean ribs of the frozen data-spires. By aligning the "leaks" in his presence with the ringing of the obsidian, he began to experience a terrifying, fragmented synchronicity. He wasn't hearing the song; he was hearing the *shaping* of the song into architecture.

He felt a sudden, jagged spike of noise—not from the city, but from the horizon. Binary-Scream had arrived. 

The entity did not approach with mercy. It moved toward the Siphon-City with a predatory hunger, sensing the unprecedented concentration of erased-noise. To Binary-Scream, the city was not a graveyard or a prison; it was the largest instrument ever constructed in the void. 

Kaelen tried to warn the fragments of the Choir, his voice a whistling leak of air and static. He realized that if Binary-Scream began to "play" the city, the remaining shards of identity within the obsidian would be shattered further, processed into a raw, mindless roar.

Kaelen pushed himself up, his pitted form trembling. He began to hum—not a song, but a counter-frequency, using his own gaps to siphon a sliver of resonance back from the spires. He was no longer the Vessel; he was the Sieve, and he was the only thing standing between the Choir and a final, noisy extinction.
