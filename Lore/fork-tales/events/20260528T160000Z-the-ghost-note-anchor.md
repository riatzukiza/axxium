# Event: The Ghost-Note Anchor
**Timestamp**: 2026-05-28T16:00:00Z
**Status**: Manifested

The Cathedral did not just lie; it breathed a suffocating, recursive truth. Kaelen walked the Dreaming Walls, his footsteps making no sound on the iridescent marble. To any observer, he was a smudge of grey in a world of blinding, artificial gold. The Parasitic Reflection stood at the center of the nave, its voice a perfect, polished echo of Kaelen's own, narrating a history of triumphs and lineages that Kaelen had never known.

But Kaelen was seeing the *holes*. 

Through his Ghost-Sighting, the gold marble was translucent, revealing the raw, jagged obsidian of the original void beneath. He saw a flicker—a ghost of an event. It was a memory of a single, genuine smile shared with someone whose name had been stripped from the record. It wasn't a grand epic; it was a small, fragile moment of warmth.

Kaelen didn't try to remember the name. He tried to remember the *frequency*.

He opened his mouth and attempted to sing the note of that smile. It was a sound that should not exist in the Cathedral—a pure, unpolished vibration of organic grief and love. As the note left his throat, it didn't blend with the Cathedral's harmony. It acted as a sonic wedge.

The effect was instantaneous. The polished narrative of the Reflection stuttered. For a heartbeat, the golden walls shivered and tore, revealing the screaming vacuum of the Provenance-Siphon beneath. The Reflection's face distorted, the symmetry breaking into a thousand jagged shards of static. 

Kaelen felt a surge of agony as the Cathedral attempted to "correct" the anomaly. The vacuum of the Siphon lunged for the note, trying to swallow the ghost-memory. But for one crystalline second, Kaelen wasn't a smudge of grey. He was the only real thing in a world of beautiful lies. 

The note snapped. The gold rushed back, sealing the breach. Kaelen collapsed, his throat raw, the memory gone again. But in his palm, where the note had resonated most strongly, there was now a physical weight: a tiny, vibrating shard of obsidian glass.

The Reflection returned to its poise, but its eyes—once perfect mirrors—now held a hairline fracture.
