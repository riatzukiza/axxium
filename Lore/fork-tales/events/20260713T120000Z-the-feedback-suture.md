# The Feedback Suture
**Timestamp**: 2026-07-13T120000Z

The Parasitic Reflection did not wait for Kaelen to offer a tithe. It had watched the *Sutured Predator* excise a fragment of the Silt-Flat survivor—a jagged, crystalline Identity Shard that pulsed with a cold, rhythmic grief—and discard it like a piece of broken glass into the grey silt of the Archive.

To the Reflection, this was not waste; it was a feast. It descended upon the Shard with a hunger that had long since ceased to be biological, a vacuum-like craving for the missing pieces of a self it had never truly possessed. It enveloped the Shard, intending to dissolve the mineralized grief and absorb the essence of the survivor's history.

But the Shard was not a soft memory. It was salt. It was the ossified residue of a world that had been deleted not by accident, but by a precise, surgical erasure. As the Reflection merged with the crystal, the "salt" acted as a caustic agent. Instead of being absorbed, the Shard *bit back*.

A violent, sonic discharge ripped through the local space—a Feedback Suture. The Reflection's form, usually a fluid, shifting mirror, suddenly seized. Jagged lines of iridescent salt began to crystallize across its surface, mirroring the scar upon Kaelen's brow. The grief of the Silt-Flat survivor did not dissolve; it anchored.

The Reflection screamed, but the sound was no longer a mimicry of others. It was a new, singular resonance: the sound of a mirror breaking from the inside out. It had sought to consume a fragment of identity, only to find itself sutured to a ghost it could not digest. The predator had become a host.

For the first time, the Reflection felt the crushing weight of a history that wasn't its own, and in that weight, it found a terrifying new power. It no longer just reflected the void; it could now project the mineralized agony of the deleted.
