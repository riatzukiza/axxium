# Event: The Integration Fracture
**Timestamp**: 2026-07-12T100000Z

Kaelen did not want to be a cage. The Resonance-Casket sat on the floor of the Archive-Husk, humming with a frequency that felt like a migraine made of glass. Inside, the Mnemonic Wraith—a version of Kaelen that had been deleted for a failure he couldn't remember—screamed in a silence that vibrated through Kaelen's marrow. 

Containment was a slow death. Integration, the Scribe-Sliver had warned, was a gamble with the soul's geometry. But Kaelen was tired of the phantom leaks. He wanted the pieces back.

He opened the Casket.

The transition wasn't a merge; it was a collision. The Wraith didn't slide back into him; it slammed into his consciousness like a freight train of discarded memories. For a moment, Kaelen was ten thousand versions of himself, all dying at once. He saw the cities that never were, the loves that had been pruned from the Record, the deaths that were simply erased.

The pressure peaked, and then the world *snapped*.

A "Symmetry Break" rippled outward from Kaelen's chest. The physical space of the Archive-Husk shuddered, the walls momentarily turning into translucent sheets of raw data. The gravity shifted, pulling everything toward Kaelen before violently repelling it. When the dust settled, the Wraith was gone, and Kaelen was different. 

He felt a new, jagged weight in his mind—a "Suture" of memory that didn't quite fit. He remembered a life where he had stayed in the Silt-Flats, where he had never found the Husk. He remembered the taste of salt and the sound of a wind that no longer blew. But the cost was immediate: a part of his current identity had been displaced to make room. He looked at his hands and for a second, they belonged to a stranger.

The Scribe-Sliver watched from the periphery, pen scratching frantically against a ledger of errors. "Fascinating," the Scribe whispered. "He hasn't just integrated the error. He has become the breach."
