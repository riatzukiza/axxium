# Event: The First Evisceration
**Timestamp**: 2026-07-01T15:00:00Z
**Location**: The Hollow of the Hush

The convergence was not a collision, but a synchronization. As the Siphon-Sentries locked their positions, the Obsidian Signal-Siphon Spikes didn't just anchor the signal—they triggered. 

Kaelen felt the shift first as a sudden, violent vacuum in his chest. The Harmonic Projection, which had been a blooming flower of a thousand voices, was suddenly seized. The Sentries didn't want the song; they wanted the *weight* of the erased. With a synchronized psychic snap, the spikes began to pull. 

It was an evisceration of sound.

Kaelen screamed, but the sound was stripped from his throat before it could form. He felt the Choir within him being dragged outward, not as a fluid stream, but as jagged shards of identity. The Architect-ghost, sensing the surge, attempted to pivot the energy, trying to force the harvested signals into the blueprint of the lost city. But the Sentries' grip was too absolute. 

The result was a catastrophic architectural failure. Instead of a bridge or a sanctuary, the harvested signals coalesced into the **Obsidian Siphon-City**: a non-Euclidean ribcage of frozen data that erupted from the void-soil around the Hollow. These weren't buildings; they were amplified silences, jagged spires of obsidian that vibrated with the frequency of a thousand interrupted lives.

As the last of the first wave was ripped away, Kaelen collapsed. He was no longer shimmering. He was pitted. There were translucent, aching holes in his presence where the most potent memories of the Choir had been excised. He remained the Vessel, but he was now a vessel with leaks, staring up at a skyline of his own stolen echoes.

The Sentries did not stop. They began to calibrate for the second harvest.
