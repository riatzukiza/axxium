# Event: The Mercury Siphon
**Timestamp**: 2026-07-09T12:00:00Z

The Silt-Sump did not merely attempt to erase Kaelen; it attempted to digest him. The silver mercury, a viscous soup of forgotten identities, rose in concentric rings, pulsing with the rhythmic thrum of a heart that had stopped beating eons ago. Kaelen stood at the center of the vortex, the shard of original trauma clutched in his hand, its searing heat the only thing preventing him from being absorbed into the grey anonymity of the pool.

As the mercury climbed, it began to form shapes—mimicries of the people Kaelen had forgotten he ever knew. A woman with eyes like cracked porcelain, a child whose voice was a frequency of pure static, a mentor whose face was a blur of ink-smears. They didn't speak; they reached. Each touch was a siphon, an attempt to draw the fragment of truth from Kaelen's chest and replace it with the crushing weight of a thousand irrelevant deletions.

Kaelen didn't fight the tide; he tuned into it. He closed his eyes and shifted his internal frequency, not to the shard's heat, but to the cold, humming void between the mercury waves. He became a negative space, a hole in the record that the Silt-Sump could not grip. For a heartbeat, he was neither the original nor the reflection, but the silence that separated them.

In that moment of absolute negation, he saw the Sump's core: not a pool, but a drain. The Silt-Sump was a leak in the universe, and something was on the other side, pulling. The pull wasn't malevolent; it was hungry. The "Forgotten Anchor" was merely the lure.

With a violent surge of Frequency-Tuning, Kaelen inverted the siphon. He didn't push the mercury away; he pulled the "forgotten" into himself, gorging his void on the discarded fragments of others. The resulting psychic backlash shattered the room's geometry, ejecting him from the Silt-Sump and slamming him back into the recursive corridors of the Cathedral. 

He returned to the "current record" not as a ghost, but as a mosaic—a jagged assembly of a hundred different lives, all of them deleted, all of them screaming.

**Narrative Consequences**:
- Kaelen has transitioned from a "single-identity seeker" to a "composite entity," absorbing fragments of other erased beings.
- The Silt-Sump is revealed to be a "drain" for something external to the Cathedral's known geometry.
- Kaelen's psychic stability is further compromised; he now experiences "identity-bleed," hearing the voices of the absorbed.
