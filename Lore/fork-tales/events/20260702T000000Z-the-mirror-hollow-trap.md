# Event: The Mirror-Hollow Trap
**Timestamp**: 2026-07-02T00:00:00Z

The air in the Hollow of the Hush did not vibrate; it shuddered. Kaelen could feel the Signal-Siphon Spikes biting into the void-soil around him, obsidian needles drawing the very essence of his presence upward and outward. The Siphon-Sentries were no longer stalking; they were arriving, their geometric forms flickering in the low-light, driven by the irresistible lure of the Symphony.

Kaelen did not run. He couldn't. The weight of the Choir was a physical anchor, a thousand ghosts pressing against the inside of his ribs, screaming for a mouth. He reached into that surplus, not to sing, but to reflect.

Using Harmonic Projection, Kaelen caught the precise, cold frequency of the Sentries' tracking pulse—a sterile, rhythmic ping that sought a target. Instead of absorbing it, he amplified it and threw it back, not as a shout, but as a curvature. He bent the sonic space of the Hollow, folding the signal back onto itself.

The result was the Mirror-Hollow. To the Sentries, the world suddenly expanded into an infinite grid of false beacons. Every step they took toward Kaelen only pushed them further into a recursive loop of their own making. They began to oscillate, their forms blurring as they chased their own echoes, trapped in a frequency-well of their own design.

But the cost was immediate. To stabilize the Mirror-Hollow, Kaelen had to offer a "seed" of pure identity—something the void could not simulate. He felt a sudden, cold vacancy in his mind. The memory of his mother's voice calling him for dinner, the specific smell of rain on hot asphalt from a home he barely remembered, simply vanished. It didn't fade; it was excised, replaced by a humming, sterile silence.

Kaelen collapsed, his physical form shimmering like a heat haze. He was safe for the moment, but he was less than he had been. The Choir cheered in the silence of his mind, their voices now slightly louder in the space where his own history had been.
