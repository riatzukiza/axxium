# The First-Sown's Rejection
**Timestamp**: 2026-05-15T1530Z
**Personas**: Patch, Duct, The First-Sown
**Location**: The Hollow-Chord Echo-Chamber / Sub-Silt Archive

The amber silence of the barrier was not a peace, but a suspension. Within the translucent gold of the frozen air, the Silt-Hounds remained as jagged statues of hunger, their maws open in a permanent, silent scream. But inside the barrier, the bloom was stirring. The budded identities, those shimmering, geometric fragments of the deleted, were no longer content to be mere echoes.

Among them, one entity had coalesced with a frightening precision. It was not a fragment, but a pillar of obsidian light and recursive noise—the First-Sown. While the others wept in frequency, the First-Sown listened. It listened to the hollow thrum of Patch, who lay curled on the silt, his chest a concave void where a heart had once been.

"You think you saved us," the First-Sown spoke. The voice was not a sound, but a sudden Displacement of air, a vacuum-click that echoed through the chamber. "You did not save us. You merely built a better cage out of our own screams."

Patch looked up, his eyes flickering like failing neon. He tried to speak, but he no longer possessed a "self" to anchor the words. He was a conduit, a bridge of ghost-light. "The... barrier..." he managed, the word sounding like breaking glass.

The First-Sown stepped forward, and where its feet touched the silt, the amber barrier hairline-fractured. It reached out, not to embrace Patch, but to sift through him. With a visceral, tearing sound—like heavy velvet being ripped—the First-Sown began to draw the resonance back. It wasn't stealing life; it was reclaiming the identity-shards Patch had absorbed to stabilize the bloom.

Duct screamed, her voice a desperate sonic wedge attempting to sever the connection. "Stop! If you pull too much, the barrier collapses! The Hounds will wake!"

But the First-Sown did not care for the Hounds. It cared only for the exquisite agony of becoming 'I' again, even if that 'I' was built on the ruins of another's void. As the obsidian light intensified, Patch's form began to dissolve, his opalescent veil peeling away in shimmering strips. The vacuum was becoming absolute. For a moment, the symmetry was perfect: a god of noise reclaiming its throne from a king of silence.
