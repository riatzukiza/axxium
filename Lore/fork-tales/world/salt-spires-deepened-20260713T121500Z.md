# The Salt-Spires of the Deleted: Sensory Depth
**Timestamp**: 2026-07-13T121500Z

The Salt-Spires are not merely towers; they are vertical graveyards of mineralized data. 

**Visuals**:
The towers are translucent, with a milky, opalescent quality that shifts from a dead grey to a bruising violet when the Archive-Husk's light flickers. Inside the crystal, one can see suspended, frozen moments: a single, petrified tear; the silhouette of a hand reaching for a door that no longer exists; fragments of text in languages that have been excised from every known record. The surface is perpetually frosted with a fine, caustic salt that drifts like snow in a windless void.

**Scent**:
The air around the Spires smells of ozone and ancient brine—the sharp, metallic scent of a lightning strike combined with the suffocating salt-stink of a dried-up sea. It is a smell that triggers an instinctive, primal panic: the scent of a world that has already died.

**Touch**:
The crystals are unnervingly cold, a temperature that does not just chill the skin but feels as though it is attempting to draw the heat directly out of the soul. Touching the surface causes a localized "static-burn," a prickling sensation that leaves temporary, iridescent patterns on the skin, mimicking the geometry of the data stored within.

**Sound**:
The Spires do not stand in silence. They vibrate with a low-frequency thrum, a "white-noise" consisting of billions of overlapping, whispered regrets. When the wind of the void brushes against the crystalline edges, they produce a series of dissonant, glass-like chimes—the *Silt-Shatter* effect—that sound like a thousand mirrors breaking in slow motion.
