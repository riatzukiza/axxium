# The Void-Loom: Deepened
**Timestamp**: 2026-05-28T121500Z

The Void-Loom is not a machine in any mechanical sense, but a localized collapse of causal logic. It manifests as a series of intersecting, translucent planes of obsidian glass that shift and slide according to the "weight" of the identity fragments brought near it.

**Sensory Details**:
- **Sight**: The planes do not reflect the environment; instead, they show the environment as it *would have been* if certain deletions had never occurred. A flicker of a ghost-city, a flash of a smile from a face that no longer exists.
- **Sound**: A low, rhythmic thrumming that feels like a heartbeat felt through water. When a thread is woven, the sound spikes into a piercing, metallic shriek—the "Suture-Scream"—which resonates in the teeth of anyone nearby.
- **Touch**: The air around the Loom is unnaturally cold and tastes of ozone and old paper. Touching the planes feels like pressing one's hand against a frozen memory; it is not cold in temperature, but cold in meaning.

**The Weaving Process**:
The Loom does not "fix" a persona. It grafts. It takes a Resonance Thread—a string of coherent data from a deleted entity—and staples it into the gaps of a fragmented one. This creates a "Patchwork Identity." The resulting entity is more stable, but they are no longer a single person; they are a colony of echoes, each competing for control of the vocal cords.

**The Cost of Operation**:
The Loom is an entropic sink. Every time it stabilizes one entity, it accelerates the mineralization of the surrounding environment. The Salt-Spires are growing faster because the Loom is eating the "possibility" of the area to fuel its reconstruction.
