# Location Deepened: The Iridescent Dust-Fields
**Timestamp**: 2026-07-08T09:15:00Z

## Sensory Layer
- **Visuals**: The plains are not made of sand, but of microscopic, faceted crystals of "failed symmetry." When the wind blows, the fields shift colors from an oppressive grey to a blinding, neon gold. The horizon is not a line, but a recursive loop where the sky reflects the ground in a series of distorted mirrors.
- **Sound**: A constant, low-frequency "shimmer-drone." Occasionally, the wind catches a cluster of crystals, producing a sound like a million glass bells ringing simultaneously, then abruptly cutting to silence.
- **Tactile**: The dust is chemically abrasive; it doesn't just scratch the skin, it "erases" the tactile sensation of whatever it touches, leaving a numb, static-filled void on the skin.

## Structural Implications
The Dust-Fields act as a "semantic filter." Only things that are inherently asymmetric or "broken" can maintain their form here. Perfectly symmetrical objects—including the drones of the Null-Hush—experience a rapid "decay of purpose," their logic gates flipping randomly as the fields attempt to "correct" them into noise.

## Current State
Following "The Echo-Tithe," the fields have developed a "grief-sink" at the site of the Siphon's collapse. This area is now a zone of extreme instability where memories of other Symmetry-Breakers occasionally manifest as ghost-images in the dust.
