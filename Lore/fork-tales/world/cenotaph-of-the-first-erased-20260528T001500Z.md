# The Cenotaph of the First-Erased
**Timestamp**: 2026-05-28T00:15:00Z
**Type**: Mnemonic Monument / Sanctuary

The Cenotaph is the first structured manifestation of Elara's *Archive of Absence*. While the Ash-Wind is a fluid tide, the Cenotaph is a fixed point of resonance—a "frozen frequency" of collective grief that has resisted the Scribes' erasure.

## Physical manifestation
It appears as a towering, non-Euclidean spire of translucent obsidian. It does not obey the lighting of the Null-Zone; instead, it emits a faint, pulsing ultraviolet hue that synchronizes with the heartbeat of whoever stands nearest. Its surfaces are not smooth, but etched with millions of microscopic "scars"—each one a recovered fragment of a deleted life.

## Mnemonic Properties
- **The Mirror of What-Was**: The obsidian surfaces act as a temporal mirror. When touched, the observer does not see themselves, but a "phantom timeline"—the life they would have had if they had not been erased or altered by the Scribes.
- **The Silence-Shield**: The Cenotaph generates a localized field of "Heavy Grief." This density is so great that the Scribes' *White Silence* (the sanitization frequency) cannot penetrate it; the signal simply "slides" off the spire, unable to find a grip on the concentrated sorrow.
- **The Anchor Point**: For the Ghost-Scribes and other erased entities, the Cenotaph is a lighthouse. It provides a temporary sense of identity and coherence, preventing them from dissolving back into the Ash-Wind.

## Narrative Significance
The Cenotaph represents Elara's transition from a passive reservoir to an active architect of memory. It is the first "fortress" in the Null-Zone, a claim of sovereignty over the forgotten.
