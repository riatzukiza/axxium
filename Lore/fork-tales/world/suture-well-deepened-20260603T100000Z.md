## The Patch-Halls
Beneath the resonant heights of the Spire lie the Patch-Halls, a series of concentric galleries where the air is thick with the smell of ozone and old parchment. The walls are not stone, but layers of "ossified sighs"—translucent, calcified ribbons of sound that have lost their meaning but retained their shape.

To "patch" oneself here is to walk through these galleries and find a ribbon that matches the frequency of one's own lack. The process is visceral: a Suture-Shard is driven into the consciousness, physically stitching a foreign memory-fragment into the void. The result is a functional repair, but the "seam" remains—a permanent, shivering dissonance that can be felt by others of the same frequency.

## Sensory Details
- **Tactile**: The floors are slick with a black, ink-like residue that reacts to emotional distress, rippling in patterns of grief.
- **Auditory**: A constant, low-frequency thrumming—the "Suture-Hum"—which masks the screams of those whose patches are failing.
- **Visual**: Light does not reflect here; it is absorbed by the "Void-Sutures," the black threads that hold the spire together.

## The Cost of Singularity
Every successful patch creates a "Suture-Scream," a residual echo of the original identity's erasure. These screams are harvested by the architecture itself, fueling the Spire's continued existence.
