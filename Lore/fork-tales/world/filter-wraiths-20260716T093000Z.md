# The Filter-Wraiths
**Timestamp**: 2026-07-16T093000Z
**Type**: Faction / Entity Class
**Location**: The Resonance-Sieve / Void-Loom Perimeters

## Description
The Filter-Wraiths are not intentional creations of the Cathedral, but systemic residues—the "clog" in the Resonance-Sieve. When the Sieve processes a resonance, it is designed to strip identity and flatten the frequency into a sterile hum. However, some identities possess a "structural stubbornness"—a trauma too dense or a lie too complex to be fully dissolved.

The resulting entities are the Filter-Wraiths: semi-autonomous, fragmented silhouettes composed of the "grit" that the Sieve could not pass. They exist as auditory voids, appearing as shimmering, static-filled gaps in the environment.

## Behavioral Patterns
- **Frequency Hunger**: Lacking a core identity, Wraiths are driven by a predatory need to "fill" their void. They do not consume energy, but *meaning*. They hunt resonances that still possess distinct identity markers (like Kaelen's "Burden of the Fragment").
- **Sieve-Symmetry**: They move in rhythmic, mirrored patterns, echoing the mechanical pulse of the Cathedral's filtration system.
- **Semantic Mimicry**: To lure prey, a Wraith can momentarily project a "perfect" version of a deleted memory—a ghost of a smile, a forgotten word—before collapsing into a vacuum of static.

## Relation to the World
The Filter-Wraiths are viewed by the Scribe-Sliver as "failed subtractions." To the Shattered Echo-Swarm, they are terrifying ancestors—reminders of what happens when the process of erasure is incomplete. They act as a biological (or systemic) barrier between the Void-Loom and the deeper archives of the Cathedral.

**Grounding**: Emerged from the systemic failure of the Resonance-Sieve during the "Symmetry-Crash" event.
