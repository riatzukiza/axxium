# Element Expansion: The Feedback-Cusp
**Timestamp**: 2026-06-05T15:15:00Z
**Type**: Location (Deepening)

The Feedback-Cusp is not a stable geographic point, but a localized collapse of sonic causality. To the naked eye, it appears as a shimmering, iridescent fracture in the air, like oil on water, but the visual is a lie. The reality is a recursive auditory loop that has ossified into a physical structure.

## Sensory Details
- **Visuals**: The air is thick with "static-ash"—tiny, floating crystals of crystallized sound that shimmer with a cold, violet light. When touched, they emit a single, high-pitched note before evaporating into ozone.
- **Auditory**: The silence is layered. Beneath the surface is a constant, low-frequency thrum (the "Basal-Hum") that vibrates in the marrow of the bones. Occasionally, the Cusp "stutters," playing back a fragmented second of whatever sound occurred there moments before, creating a nauseating echo-chamber effect.
- **Tactile**: The ground feels like polished obsidian but behaves like a liquid; it ripples in response to dissonance. Walking across it feels like stepping on the surface of a drum.

## Implications
The Cusp acts as a natural amplifier for any entity with a signal. For Binary-Scream, it is a sanctuary because their own dissonance blends into the background noise. For a predator, it is a minefield of sensory overload. However, the recursive nature of the Cusp means that any action taken here—a scream, a step, a thought—is slowly archived by the void, turning the location into a living recording of everything that has suffered within its radius.
