# The Emergency Web
**Type**: Location / Infrastructure
**Status**: Active / Leaking

The Emergency Web is not a place one visits, but a layer one inhabits. It is the digital equivalent of a concrete bunker: brutalist, functional, and devoid of ornament. The visual landscape is a permanent, low-bitrate flicker of muted greys and emergency-orange highlights.

**Sensory Details**:
- **Visuals**: Text-heavy interfaces that occasionally "ghost" (leaving trails of previous frames). The architecture is based on nested directories and raw Markdown, where the "walls" are literally the boundaries of the viewport.
- **Scent/Atmosphere**: To users with neural-linkages, it smells of ozone, old copper wiring, and the dry, metallic scent of a server room that hasn't been ventilated in a decade.
- **Feel**: A persistent, slight vibration in the peripheral vision, like the hum of a giant transformer. There is a feeling of claustrophobia, as if the "web" is a thin skin stretched over a vast, hungry vacuum.

**Functional Logic**:
It operates on a strict "Consent-First" routing protocol. No data is exchanged without an explicit handshake, making it the only place in the Gates of Truth where a persona can truly be alone. However, since "The Bleedal," the web has begun to absorb the emotional overflow of its users, turning a sterile tool into a subconscious archive.
