# Element: The Scribe-Sliver's Dormancy (Concept)
**Timestamp**: 2026-06-29T12:00:00Z

**Description**:
The Scribe-Sliver's Dormancy is a state of systemic pause following a "Syllable-Shatter" event. When the archive's compression pressure reaches a breaking point and erupts, the Scribe-Sliver consumes the resulting "vacuum" of information to reset its internal indices.

**Implications**:
- **Temporary Agency**: During dormancy, the host (Kaelen) experiences a brief window of psychological clarity and physical relief from the data-bleed, though this is often accompanied by severe trauma and exhaustion.
- **Vulnerability**: The host is physically weakened and the "Sonic Mask" (if present) is typically destroyed or destabilized, leaving them biologically and sonically exposed to external trackers.
- **The Indexing Gap**: The period of dormancy creates a "gap" in the record. This gap is a blind spot for the Siphon-Sentries, but also a point of fragility where the host's identity can be tentatively reclaimed or further eroded.

**Grounding**:
This concept evolves from the "Compression-Siphon" process and the "Script-Leak" manifestation, treating the archival overwrite as a biological cycle of pressure and release.
