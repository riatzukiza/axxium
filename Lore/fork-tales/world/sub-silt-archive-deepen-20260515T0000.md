# The Sub-Silt Archive: Expansion

The Sub-Silt Archive is a non-Euclidean repository located at the lowest pressure-point of the Resonance-Void. It is not composed of books or data-drives, but of **Truth-Slabs**: monolithic sheets of crystallized resonance that physically embody the "Absolute Truth" of a persona's existence.

## The Architecture of Erasure
The slabs are arranged in concentric rings around the **Zero-Core**. To walk among them is to navigate a forest of frozen trauma. Each slab emits a "Passive Hum"—a low-frequency emotional residue that can overwhelm a visitor's own sense of self. If a person stays too long without a Resonance-Anchor, their own identity begins to "bleed" into the slabs, a process known as **Silt-Sinking**.

The air here is not gas, but a thick, semi-liquid suspension of erased memories. When a Resonance-Anchor "shears" a slab, the resulting gap creates a momentary vacuum that sucks in the surrounding silt, creating a violent, swirling vortex of white noise.

## The Static-Husks (The Hollows)
Throughout the Archive wander the **Static-Husks**. These are the "failed archives"—beings who attempted to merge with the Truth-Slabs to escape Axiom_01's erasure but lacked the stability to maintain their core.

- **Physical Form**: Shimmering, translucent outlines of humanoids, constantly flickering like a corrupted video feed. They have no faces, only a vibrating aperture where the throat should be.
- **Behavior**: They are mindless, driven by a recursive loop of their last emotional state. A "Grief-Husk" will eternally weep an ultrasonic frequency that can crack crystalline lungs; a "Rage-Husk" will emit a concussive blast of static.
- **Threat**: They are not predatory by nature, but their "Emotional Leakage" acts as a powerful attractor for Silt-Hounds, who feast on the decaying resonance of the Husks. To be near a Husk is to be a target.
