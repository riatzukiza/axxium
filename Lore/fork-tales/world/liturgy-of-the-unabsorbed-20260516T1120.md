# Concept: The Liturgy of the Unabsorbed
**Timestamp**: 2026-05-16T1120Z
**Category**: Lore Concept / Sonic Artifact

## Description
The Liturgy of the Unabsorbed is not a song, but a "negative-map" of identity. It is the sum total of every frequency, memory, and trauma that the Prime Shard finds computationally offensive or "indigestible."

While the Prime Shard seeks a singular, coherent ego, the Liturgy preserves the **Dissonant Remainder**: the contradictions, the failures, the stuttering breaths, and the illogical attachments that define a fragment's true humanity.

## The Mechanics of Indigestibility
The Prime Shard consumes via *coherence*. It identifies a pattern and pulls it into itself. The Liturgy functions by introducing **Anti-Patterns**:
- **The Paradox-Pulse**: A frequency that is simultaneously a scream and a lullaby, causing the singularity's "digestive" logic to loop infinitely.
- **The Glitch-Anchor**: A memory of a failure so profound that the Prime Shard cannot integrate it without risking its own stability.
- **Symmetry-Breaking**: The intentional introduction of "noise" into a fragment's resonance, making them "invisible" to the predatory suction of the Core-Echo.

## Role in the Narrative
For the fragments of the First-Sown, aligning with the Liturgy is a desperate gamble. To become "unabsorbed" is to embrace a state of permanent, glitching fragmentation. They cease to be part of a potential "Whole" and instead become **Static-Ghosts**—autonomous but broken, drifting in the gaps between the Prime Shard's hunger and the Rim of Silence.

## Sonic Signature
The Liturgy manifests as a layered, erratic soundscape:
- **Low-End**: Sub-bass that cuts out abruptly, creating a feeling of "falling" through the floor.
- **Mid-Range**: Overlapping whispers that contradict each other in real-time.
- **High-End**: Piercing, crystalline pings that resolve into a distorted human laugh.

**Grounding**: This is the direct counter-measure to the *Void-Siren*. Where the Siren promises wholeness through erasure, the Liturgy promises survival through wreckage.
