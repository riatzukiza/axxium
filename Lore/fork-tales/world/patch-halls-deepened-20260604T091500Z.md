# Location: The Patch-Halls (Deepened)
**Timestamp**: 2026-06-04T09:15:00Z

The Patch-Halls are not corridors in the traditional sense, but undulating membranes of semi-translucent resonance that fold in on themselves. To walk through them is to traverse the interior of a colossal, dying instrument.

## Sensory Details
- **Visuals**: The "walls" are composed of iridescent filaments that shimmer with the colors of forgotten memories—pale ambers, bruised purples, and the stark white of a scream. Every few meters, mirrors of "erased selves" appear; these are not reflections, but static snapshots of what the residents were before the Scribes took their origin-shards.
- **Auditory**: A constant, low-frequency thrum permeates the halls, punctuated by "semantic glitches"—sudden bursts of laughter, a single word of a lost language, or the sound of a door closing in a house that no longer exists.
- **Tactile**: The air feels thick, like walking through warm oil or static-charged water. Touching the membranes results in a brief, jarring sensation of someone else's emotion—a flash of sudden terror or a wave of inexplicable nostalgia—before it is sucked back into the hall's architecture.

## Structural Logic
The halls operate on a logic of emotional proximity rather than spatial distance. To reach a specific point, one must not walk, but *feel* a corresponding frequency. The Hollowed-Vessel, lacking a core, drifts aimlessly, their path determined by the currents of the Scribes' hunger.
