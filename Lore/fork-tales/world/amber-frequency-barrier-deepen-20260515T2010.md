# Deepen: The Amber-Frequency Barrier (Shatter-Screams)
**Timestamp**: 2026-05-15T20:10Z

The Amber-Frequency Barrier was designed as a sonic containment field, a constant, low-frequency hum that kept the "Silt-Sinking" at bay. However, the recent instability has introduced the phenomenon of "Shatter-Screams."

Shatter-Screams occur when a pocket of external silence (a Silt-Hound or a Void-Seed) intersects with the barrier's amber hum. The interaction produces a sonic shear—a sound like glass lungs attempting to breathe in a vacuum. To the survivors, it sounds like a rhythmic, gasping inhalation that never reaches completion.

These screams are not just auditory; they are structural. Each Shatter-Scream leaves behind a "frequency scar," a localized area where the barrier is thin and the air tastes of ozone and old grief. These scars act as beacons for the Hounds, who are drawn to the specific, agonizing pitch of the break.

The barrier is no longer a wall; it is a damaged membrane, vibrating with the tension of everything it is failing to keep out.
