# Element: The Obsidian-Silt Basin (Location)
**Timestamp**: 2026-06-29T12:00:00Z

**Description**:
The Obsidian-Silt Basin is a low-lying geographical depression where the residue of "Syllable-Shatters" accumulates. It is a landscape of black, powdery silt that is actually micronized archival ink. Walking through the basin creates "acoustic ghosts"—temporary, audible fragments of memories that were discarded during the compression process.

**Sensory Details**:
- **Visual**: A vast, monochrome plain of shimmering black dust that ripples like liquid under the wind.
- **Auditory**: A constant, low-frequency hum (the "Silt-Hum") punctuated by sharp, crystalline whispers of forgotten names and fragmented sentences.
- **Olfactory**: The smell of ozone, old ink, and wet stone.
- **Tactile**: The silt is unnervingly cold and clings to skin and fabric like a magnetic fluid.

**Grounding**:
The basin acts as the natural sink for the environmental erosion caused by the Scribe-Sliver's presence, grounding the "Script-Haze" into a physical, permanent geography.
