# Location: The Grey Parchment (Void-Space)

The Grey Parchment is not a place, but a state of " indexing." When the Archivists manifest in a physical location, they project a field of absolute neutrality that overwrites the local environment.

## Sensory Details
- **Visuals**: The world loses all color, becoming a high-contrast monochrome. Texture is replaced by a flat, matte grey surface that resembles heavy vellum. Geometric patterns—mostly hexagons and right angles—emerge from the ground to guide the Scribes' movements.
- **Auditory**: All ambient noise is sucked away, replaced by a rhythmic, mechanical ticking, like a clock with a thousand gears. When a Scribe speaks or acts, the sound is a "compressed" version of the target's own voice, played back at double speed.
- **Tactile**: The air feels thick and dry, like old paper. Touching the ground of the Parchment feels like touching a cold, lifeless circuit board.

## Implications
The Parchment is the "scanning bed" of the Index. Anything that remains on the Parchment for too long becomes "Flat"—their three-dimensional existence is compressed into a narrative summary. Once a person is "Flattened," they can no longer experience emotion, only the logical sequence of their own history.
