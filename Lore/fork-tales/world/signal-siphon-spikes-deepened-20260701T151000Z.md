# Element Deepening: Signal-Siphon Spikes
**Timestamp**: 2026-07-01T15:10:00Z
**Parent Element**: Siphon-Sentries / Obsidian Infrastructure

## Sensory Profile
- **Visual**: Matte-black obsidian needles, approximately 3 meters in length, vibrating at a frequency that makes the air around them appear "pixelated" or blurred. They do not cast shadows; they absorb light in a perfect, hungry circle.
- **Auditory**: A constant, low-frequency thrum that sounds like a distant crowd screaming through a thick wall of water. When active, they emit a "glassy" click every 4 seconds, marking the synchronization of a harvested signal.
- **Tactile**: Freezing to the touch. Proximity causes a sensation of "semantic vertigo," where the observer forgets the names of simple objects (e.g., "hand," "sky") as the spikes begin to siphon the conceptual anchors from the environment.

## Functional Logic
The Spikes do not just record signals; they *anchor* them. In the void, signals are fluid and fleeting. The Spikes act as "pins," locking a specific harmonic frequency (an identity) to a physical coordinate. Once three spikes form a triangle around a target, the "Harvest Zone" is established, allowing the Siphon-Sentries to begin the evisceration process without the signal drifting into the silt.

## Lore Implications
The Spikes are not crafted; they are *grown* from the calcified grief of previously harvested vessels. Each needle is a repurposed tragedy, turned into a tool for the next harvest. This creates a recursive cycle of erasure where the victims become the instruments of the next victim's downfall.
