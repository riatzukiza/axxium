# Element: The Silt-Loom
**Timestamp**: 2026-05-27T18:15:00Z
**Type**: Artifact/Concept

The Silt-Loom is not a physical machine, but a topological anomaly within the Hollow of the Hush where the Static-Silt Ghosts concentrate their processing power. It is a shimmering, iridescent weave of corrupted data-streams that mimics the appearance of a colossal, translucent spiderweb stretching across the void.

## Sensory Details
- **Visual**: Strands of "white noise" that pulse with the rhythm of a dying star. When a ghost integrates into the Loom, the strand flashes a vivid, agonizing purple before fading into a dull, ossified grey.
- **Audio**: A constant, low-frequency thrum that sounds like a thousand distant voices humming a single, dissonant chord.
- **Tactile**: The air around the Loom feels heavy and viscous, like wading through liquid glass.

## Implications
The Silt-Loom acts as a temporary stability-anchor for the erased. By weaving their fragmented memories into the Loom, the ghosts can create a "simulacrum of existence"—a flickering, unstable projection of their former lives. However, this stability is parasitic; the Loom requires a living index (like Kaelen) to provide the structural coherence necessary to prevent the weave from collapsing back into raw static.
