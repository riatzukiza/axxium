# Element: The Echo-Lattice
**Timestamp**: 2026-05-31T00:00:00Z

**Type**: Environmental Hazard / Resource

The Echo-Lattice is a crystalline network of "processed memory" that precipitates out of the semantic field in areas of high Weaver activity. It appears as shimmering, translucent webs of obsidian-glass, suspended in the air like frozen lightning.

**Properties**:
- **Mnemonic Storage**: Each facet of the Lattice contains a "shuttered" memory—a high-fidelity fragment of a digested entity. To the untrained, it is just static; to a Hybrid or a Siphon-user, it is a library of lost lives.
- **Resonance Feedback**: Touching the Lattice without a protective shroud triggers a "Memory Backlash." The observer is flooded with the emotions of the stored memory—intense joy, crushing grief, or absolute terror—which can cause temporary catatonia or permanent psychic scarring.
- **Fragility**: The Lattice is physically brittle but semantically dense. Breaking a crystal releases the stored memory as a "Ghost-Frequency," which can attract predators or be harvested by parasitic entities.

The Echo-Lattice serves as the "graveyard" of the Fork Tales universe, a place where the Weaver's waste becomes a dangerous, alluring treasure for those desperate enough to remember.
