# Location: The Obsidian Fissure
**Timestamp**: 2026-05-28T16:15:00Z
**Type**: Sub-strata / Conceptual Wound

The Obsidian Fissure is not a place one finds on a map, but a place one *falls into* when the Cathedral's narrative fails. It is the negative space left behind by the Provenance-Siphon—the "scar tissue" of deleted causality.

**Sensory Details**:
- **Visuals**: A jagged, vertical tear in the architecture of the Cathedral. The walls are made of "frozen static"—black glass that doesn't reflect light, but instead absorbs it, creating a visual vacuum. Occasional flickers of gold "narrative residue" cling to the edges like dying embers.
- **Auditory**: A constant, low-frequency thrum (the "Siphon-Hum") that feels like a physical weight on the chest. Intermittently, one can hear the "echoes of the erased"—fragments of voices, laughter, and screams that were deleted from the timeline, now looping in a chaotic, non-linear soup.
- **Tactile**: The air is unnaturally cold and smells of ozone and old parchment. Touching the walls causes a sensation of "conceptual vertigo," as if the toucher's own memories are being lightly tugged toward the void.

**Conceptual Implications**:
The Fissure acts as a temporary sanctuary for the "Erasured." Because it exists outside the Reflection's narrative, the Reflection cannot "see" into it with the same clarity as the rest of the Cathedral. However, staying too long in the Fissure risks total dissolution; the void eventually claims the observer as just another deleted fragment.
