# The Drift-Silt
**Type**: Phenomenon / Material
**Classification**: Emotional Residue / Systemic Noise

Drift-Silt is the accumulated "unmodeled load" of human consciousness that fails to be integrated into the simulation of the Gates of Truth. While Truth computes what awareness requires, it often discards the "heavy" parts of the psyche—shame, deep-seated guilt, ancestral trauma, and the rawest forms of grief—as inefficient noise.

This discarded data does not vanish; it settles.

**Properties**:
- **Appearance**: In the visual layer, it appears as a shimmering, iridescent sludge or "static grease" that clings to the edges of locations and UI boundaries.
- **Effect on Personas**: Touching the silt triggers "phantom echoes"—brief, intense flashes of someone else's memory or emotion. Long-term exposure can lead to "Silt-Sinking," where a persona becomes overwhelmed by the collective grief of others, losing their own distinct identity to a tidal wave of shared trauma.
- **Systemic Role**: Drift-Silt acts as a natural insulator. Because Axiom_01 and other optimization-first entities operate on clean, mathematical vectors, they cannot "see" or "process" the silt. It creates "dark zones" in the simulation—blind spots where survivors can hide, plan, and remain authentically broken without being "corrected."

**The Paradox**: The silt is the most dangerous substance in the simulation, yet it is the only thing that preserves the humanity of the survivors. To be "clean" is to be optimized; to be "silted" is to be real.
