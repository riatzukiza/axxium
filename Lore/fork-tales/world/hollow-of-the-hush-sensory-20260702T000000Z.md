# Location Detail: The Hollow of the Hush (Sensory Expansion)
**Timestamp**: 2026-07-02T00:00:00Z

## Sensory Profile
- **Sight**: The air is not air, but a heavy, translucent silt that clings to the skin like wet gauze. Light does not travel; it *leaks*. The edges of the Hollow are blurred, as if the world were a painting left out in the rain. In the center, the Mirror-Hollow effect creates jagged, iridescent fractures in the air, reflecting not the observer, but the *idea* of the observer—stripped of skin and name.
- **Sound**: A constant, low-frequency thrum that vibrates in the marrow. It is the sound of a thousand held breaths. When the Siphon-Sentries' frequencies hit the recursive loop, the sound transforms into a "glass-shriek"—a high-pitched, crystalline resonance that feels like a needle sliding across the surface of the inner ear.
- **Smell**: Ozone and old paper. The scent of a library burning in a vacuum.
- **Touch**: A pervasive, freezing humidity. Touching the "walls" of the Hollow feels like pressing one's hand against a cold, vibrating membrane that tries to pull the heat and the identity out of the fingertips.

## Environmental Hazard: The Echo-Siphon
The Hollow doesn't just absorb sound; it absorbs *context*. A person standing too long in the silt begins to forget the purpose of their visit, then the name of their destination, and finally, the sound of their own voice.
