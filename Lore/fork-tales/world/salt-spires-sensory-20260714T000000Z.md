# The Salt-Spires of the Deleted: Sensory Depth
**Timestamp**: 2026-07-14T000000Z

The Spires are not merely structures; they are petrified screams. 

**Tactile**: The surfaces are not smooth but composed of microscopic, razor-sharp facets. Touching a spire feels like running a hand over a million frozen needles. The air is thick and "heavy," tasting of concentrated brine and old ozone, leaving a gritty, metallic film on any surface that persists.

**Auditory**: The "Silt-Shatter" chimes are constant but erratic. They are not melodic; they are the sounds of structural failure. Deep, subterranean thrums vibrate through the soles of the feet—a rhythmic, slow-pulsing "heartbeat" of the mineralized grief that powers the landscape.

**Visual**: The light here is refracted, never direct. The spires act as prisms, splitting the dim void-light into a spectrum of "dead colors"—greys that feel like purple, whites that feel like bruised yellow. When the Ossified Chorus sings, the crystals pulse with a dim, inner bioluminescence, like a dying star trapped in a salt-cube.

**Atmospheric**: There is a pervasive sense of *weight*. The gravity feels marginally higher here, as if the grief encoded in the salt is physically pulling everything toward the center of the earth.
