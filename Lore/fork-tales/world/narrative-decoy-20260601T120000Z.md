# Element: The Narrative Decoy (Scribe-Tactic)
**Timestamp**: 2026-06-01T12:00:00Z

**Type**: Technique / Conceptual Artifact

The Narrative Decoy is an advanced application of Scribe-Sight. Rather than altering the physical world, the user creates a "semantic anomaly"—a high-density point of interest in the world's metadata that mimics the signature of a specific entity or state.

**Mechanics**:
- **Metadata Projection**: The user projects a specific "story-tag" (e.g., *Fragile*, *High-Value*, *Secret*) into the lattice of the world.
- **Identity Shredding**: To make a decoy believable to a narrative predator like the Lattice-Stalker, the decoy must contain a genuine fragment of the user's identity. The user "sheds" a piece of their own coherence to anchor the lie.
- **Semantic Hunger**: Predators are drawn to coherence. A decoy that feels "real" is more attractive than the true, obscured target.

**Implications**:
- **The Cost of Deception**: Every decoy created reduces the user's total coherence. Over time, the user becomes a "Hollow-Shell," a creature of pure projection with no internal core.
- **Predatory Training**: Predators that encounter decoys may evolve, learning to distinguish between "Scribe-Lies" and "True-Identities," leading to a "Semantic Arms Race."

The Narrative Decoy is a powerful survival tool, but it effectively trades the soul for a few moments of safety.
