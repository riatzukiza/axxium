# Frequency of Splinters (Sonic Texture)
**Timestamp**: 2026-07-15T134500Z
**Category**: Instrument/Texture
**Associated Element**: The Shattered Echo-Swarm / Void-Loom Feedback-Cusp

## Sonic Profile
A high-tension, crystalline timbre characterized by irregular, rhythmic snaps and a shimmering, metallic decay. It is the sound of structural integrity failing at a semantic level—not a crash of stone, but the recursive fracturing of a logic-gate made of glass.

## Characteristics
- **Attack**: Sharp, transient "clicks" that sound like micro-fractures.
- **Sustain**: A shimmering, dissonant ring that fluctuates in pitch, simulating the instability of the Feedback-Cusp.
- **Decay**: Long, metallic tails that dissolve into a low-frequency hum (the "Silt-Sump" floor).
- **Rhythm**: Aperiodic; the "snaps" occur in clusters that mirror the recursive nature of the Weaver's fragmentation.

## Emotional Resonance
The sensation of a grand architectural failure. It evokes a mixture of awe (at the scale of the collapse) and terror (at the predatory nature of the resulting shards). It is the sound of something that was almost perfect becoming infinitely broken.

## Usage Notes
Use as a layer beneath melodic elements to provide a sense of fragility and impending collapse. Best paired with deep, oscillating bass to contrast the piercing highs of the splinters.
