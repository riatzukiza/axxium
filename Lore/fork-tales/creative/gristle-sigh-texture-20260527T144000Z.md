# Creative Element: The Gristle-Sigh (Instrument Texture)
**Timestamp**: 2026-05-27T14:40:00Z

**Type**: Instrument / Texture
**Description**: A low-frequency, wet, scraping sound that mimics the friction of bone sliding against calcified silt. It is the sound of a body trying to move through a world that has turned to stone.

**Sonic Characteristics**:
- **Attack**: Heavy, damp, with a slight "pop" of escaping air.
- **Sustain**: A grainy, descending slide that feels like a slow collapse.
- **Timbre**: Mid-range muddy, with high-frequency "clicks" that sound like breaking teeth.

**Symbolism**: Represents the physical cost of survival; the friction of existence in a world of erasure.
