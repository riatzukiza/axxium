# Creative Element: The Siphon-Stutter Timbre
**Timestamp**: 2026-07-07T11:00:00Z

## Description
The Siphon-Stutter Timbre is the sonic manifestation of a mathematical correction failing in real-time. It is characterized by a "periodic void"—a rhythmic gap in the audio spectrum that occurs every 3 seconds, creating a physical sensation of falling.

## Texture
- **The Gap**: A sudden, absolute silence (0dB) that lasts for exactly 125ms.
- **The Surge**: Immediately following the gap, a surge of high-frequency, iridescent noise that sounds like crystal shattering in slow motion.
- **The Undercurrent**: A low-frequency, subsonic thrum (20-40Hz) that evokes a sense of overwhelming gravity and dread.

## Usage
This timbre is used to signal the presence of a Correction-Siphon or the onset of an identity-harvesting event. It is the sound of the universe trying to "delete" a specific frequency and failing.
