# Creative Element: The Memory-Sieve Timbre
**Timestamp**: 2026-06-22T12:00:00Z
**Category**: Instrument / Texture

**Description**: 
The "Memory-Sieve Timbre" is a sonic manifestation of the extraction process. It sounds like a high-frequency crystalline chime that is immediately followed by a wet, sucking vacuum. It is the sound of something being pulled out of a liquid—a sharp *ping* of recognition, then a sudden *shloop* of absence.

**Sonic Characteristics**:
- **Attack**: Sharp, metallic, almost painful.
- **Sustain**: A rapid decay into a granular hiss.
- **Tail**: A low-frequency thrum that feels like it is pulling the listener's center of gravity toward the sound source.

**Symbolic Meaning**: 
It represents the transition from "known" to "lost." In a composition, this timbre should be used to mark the exact moment a character forgets something crucial.
