# Creative Element: The Bone-Click Timbre
**Timestamp**: 2026-06-08T10:15:00Z

**Element**: Instrument / Texture
**Description**: The "Bone-Click" is a percussive, dry timbre derived from the synchronized snapping of calcified husk joints within the Silt-Mound Spire. 

**Sonic Characteristics**:
- **Attack**: Immediate, sharp, and high-frequency. Like a dry twig snapping or a heavy stone clicking against a hollow shell.
- **Decay**: Extremely short, with a metallic "ring" that suggests the material is more mineral than biological.
- **Emotional Quality**: Clinical, rhythmic, and devoid of warmth. It evokes the feeling of a clock ticking down in a room made of ribs.

**Integration**:
This timbre is used in the "Silt-Husk" themes to represent the mechanical, unthinking nature of the Spire's collective. It contrasts sharply with the fluid, sighing drones of the void, acting as a rhythmic anchor that signals the approach of the fused mass.
