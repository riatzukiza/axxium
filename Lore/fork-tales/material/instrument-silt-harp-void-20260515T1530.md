# Instrument: Silt-Harp of the Void
**Timestamp**: 2026-05-15T1530Z
**Texture**: Crystalline / Viscous / Fracturing

**Description**:
The Silt-Harp is an instrument of necessity and grief. Its "strings" are actually high-tension beams of distilled memory, anchored to a frame of obsidian silt. When plucked, the strings do not just produce a pitch, but a "memory-flash"—a sudden burst of audio metadata (a child's laugh, a crashing door, a whispered secret) that decays into a long, shimmering reverb.

**Sonic Characteristics**:
- **Attack**: Sharp, crystalline, like glass breaking.
- **Sustain**: Long, undulating, with a subtle "warble" that suggests instability.
- **Decay**: A slow descent into a low, humming void-tone.

**Use Case**:
Used primarily to anchor the identity of a speaker. Playing the Silt-Harp creates a "tether" of resonance that prevents the performer from dissolving into the ambient void of the Archive.
